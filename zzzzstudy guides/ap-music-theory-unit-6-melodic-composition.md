## üéµ 6.1 Melodic Structure

### Melodic Contour

```
CONTOUR: The shape of a melody

ASCENDING: Rising motion
C ‚Üí D ‚Üí E ‚Üí F ‚Üí G

DESCENDING: Falling motion
G ‚Üí F ‚Üí E ‚Üí D ‚Üí C

WAVE: Alternating motion
C ‚Üí E ‚Üí D ‚Üí F ‚Üí E ‚Üí G

```

### Melodic Range

```
RANGE: Distance from lowest to highest

NARROW RANGE (5th or less):
Easy to sing
Folk songs, children's music

MODERATE RANGE (octave):
Most common
Standard vocal range

WIDE RANGE (more than octave):
More dramatic
Instrumental or virtuosic

TESSITURA:
The "comfortable" range
Where most notes lie
May differ from full range
```

### Melodic Motion Types

```
TYPES OF MOTION:

CONJUNCT (Stepwise):
Moving by 2nds
C ‚Üí D ‚Üí E ‚Üí D ‚Üí C
Smooth, singable

DISJUNCT (Leaps):
Moving by 3rd or larger
C ‚Üí G ‚Üí E ‚Üí C ‚Üí G
More dramatic, angular

BALANCED MELODY:
Mix of steps and leaps
Leap followed by step (often)
Creates interest with singability
```

## üìù 6.4 Phrase Structure

### Phrases and Periods

```
PHRASE:
Musical "sentence"
Usually 4-8 measures
Ends with cadence

PERIOD:
Two phrases that form a unit

ANTECEDENT PHRASE:
First phrase ("question")
Usually ends with HC or IAC

CONSEQUENT PHRASE:
Second phrase ("answer")
Usually ends with PAC
```

### Period Types

```
PARALLEL PERIOD:
Antecedent and consequent start similarly

‚îÇ a ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ HC ‚îÇ a ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ PAC ‚îÇ

‚îÇ a ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ HC ‚îÇ b ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ PAC ‚îÇ
Different beginnings

SYMMETRIC PERIOD:
Equal phrase lengths (4+4, 8+8)

ASYMMETRIC:
Unequal phrase lengths (4+6, etc.)
```

### Phrase Expansion

```
PHRASE EXTENSION TECHNIQUES:

REPETITION:
Repeat a motive or figure

SEQUENCE:
Repeat pattern at different pitch

FRAGMENTATION:
Repeat smaller portion of motive

EXTENSION:
Add measures before cadence

ELISION:
End of one phrase = start of next
Overlapping phrases
```

### Sentence Structure

```
SENTENCE:
Different from period
Single phrase with internal logic

PRESENTATION (mm. 1-4):
Motive + repetition/sequence

CONTINUATION (mm. 5-8):
```

## ‚úçÔ∏è 6.6 Melodic Composition

### Melody Writing Guidelines

```
COMPOSING EFFECTIVE MELODIES:

1. ESTABLISH KEY
   Begin and end on tonic
   Or end phrase on dominant

2. BALANCE MOTION
   Mix steps and leaps
   Compensate large leaps

3. CLIMAX
   Build to high point
   Usually in second half

4. SHAPE
   Clear contour
   Avoid rambling

5. CADENTIAL MOTION
   Strong endings
   2-1 or 7-1 at cadence
```

### Melodic Interval Guidelines

```
INTERVAL USAGE:

PREFERRED:
- 2nds (steps) most common
- 3rds very singable
- P4, P5 for leaps
- P8 occasionally (dramatic)

AVOID:
- A2 (awkward to sing)
- A4/d5 (tritone) - unstable
- 7ths (difficult)
- Consecutive large leaps

AFTER LARGE LEAP:
Move by step in opposite direction
(Compensatory motion)
```

### Using Non-Chord Tones

```
ADDING NCTs TO MELODY:

1. WRITE STRUCTURAL MELODY
   Chord tones on strong beats
   Clear harmonic outline

2. ADD EMBELLISHMENTS
   Passing tones between
   Neighbors for decoration

3. CONSIDER RHYTHM
   NCTs often shorter values
   Main tones on beats

4. DON'T OVERDO
   Too many NCTs = chaos
   Balance is key
```

## üìè 6.7 Harmonizing Melodies

### Melody-Harmony Relationship

```
DETERMINING HARMONY FROM MELODY:

1. IDENTIFY CHORD TONES
   Notes on strong beats
   Suggest chord choice

2. CHECK BASS MOTION
   Root movement options
   Smooth bass line

3. CONSIDER FUNCTION
   Phrase beginning = T
   Pre-cadence = S or D
   Cadence = V ‚Üí I

EXAMPLE:
Melody: E - D - C (in C major)
Beat 1: E = chord tone of C or Am
Beat 2: D = chord tone of G or Dm
Beat 3: C = chord tone of C or Am
Progression: I - V - I or I - V - vi
```

### Implied Harmony

```
MELODY IMPLIES HARMONY:

ARPEGGIATED FIGURES:
C-E-G implies C major chord

SCALE PASSAGES:
May pass through multiple harmonies
Chord tones on strong beats

NCTs DON'T DEFINE CHORD:
Passing tone on beat 3
Doesn't determine harmony
Look for structural tones
```
