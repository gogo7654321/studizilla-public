# üåç Unit 5: Revolutions (1750-1900)

## üìã Unit Overview

The period from 1750-1900 was an age of revolutions - political, intellectual, and industrial. Enlightenment ideas challenged traditional authority, inspiring revolutions across the Atlantic world. These movements reshaped governments, societies, and economies, establishing principles that continue to influence the world today! üî•

| Question | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| How did Enlightenment ideas spread? | Philosophy, print culture, salons |

| How did revolutions differ? | Goals, participants, outcomes |
| What was nationalism? | New form of political identity |
| How did industrialization transform society? | Economic and social changes |

| Revolution | Dates | Location | Outcome |
|------------|-------|----------|---------|

## üí° The Enlightenment

### Core Ideas

The Enlightenment was an intellectual movement that emphasized reason, individualism, and skepticism of traditional authority.

| Concept | Meaning | Significance |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Reason** | Human ability to understand | Challenge to faith-based authority |
| **Natural rights** | Rights inherent to humans | Basis for revolution |
| **Social contract** | Government by consent | Legitimacy from people |
| **Progress** | Society can improve | Optimism about change |
| **Secularism** | Separation from religion | Church authority questioned |

### Major Enlightenment Thinkers

| Thinker | Key Ideas | Influence |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **John Locke** | Natural rights (life, liberty, property); consent; right to revolution | American Declaration; all revolutions |
| **Montesquieu** | Separation of powers; checks and balances | US Constitution |
| **Voltaire** | Religious tolerance; freedom of speech | First Amendment concepts |
| **Jean-Jacques Rousseau** | Social contract; general will; popular sovereignty | French Revolution |
| **Adam Smith** | Free markets; "invisible hand"; laissez-faire | Capitalism |
| **Mary Wollstonecraft** | Women's rights; education | Feminist movements |

### John Locke (1632-1704)

| Concept | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Natural rights** | Life, liberty, property inherent |
| **Tabula rasa** | Mind is blank slate; environment shapes us |
| **Consent** | Government must have people's consent |
| **Right to revolt** | Against tyranny |
| **Influence** | Declaration of Independence; all liberal movements |

### Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712-1778)

| Concept | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Social contract** | Agreement between people and government |
| **General will** | Common good of community |
| **Popular sovereignty** | Power resides in people |
| **"Man is born free"** | Society corrupts natural goodness |
| **Influence** | French Revolution; democratic theory |

### Spread of Enlightenment Ideas

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Print culture** | Books, newspapers, pamphlets |
| **Salons** | Intellectual gatherings in homes |
| **Coffeehouses** | Public discussion spaces |
| **Secret societies** | Freemasons spread ideas |
| **Correspondence** | Letters between intellectuals |
| **Encyclop√©die** | Diderot's comprehensive work |

## üá´üá∑ The French Revolution (1789-1799)

### Causes

| Cause | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Financial crisis** | Debt from wars (including American) |
| **Social inequality** | Three estates system |
| **Food crisis** | Bad harvests; high bread prices |
| **Enlightenment** | Ideas spread among educated |
| **American example** | Proved revolution possible |
| **Weak leadership** | Louis XVI indecisive |

### The Three Estates

| Estate | Composition | Population | Privileges |
|--------|-------------|------------|------------|
| **First** | Clergy | <1% | Tax exempt; owned 10% of land |
| **Second** | Nobility | 2% | Tax exempt; controlled offices |
| **Third** | Everyone else | 97% | Paid taxes; few rights |

### Phases of the Revolution

#### Phase 1: Liberal Revolution (1789-1791)

| Event | Date | Significance |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **Estates-General** | May 1789 | King called assembly |

| **Great Fear** | Summer 1789 | Peasant uprisings |
| **August Decrees** | Aug 4, 1789 | Feudalism abolished |
| **Declaration of Rights** | Aug 26, 1789 | Rights proclaimed |
| **March on Versailles** | Oct 1789 | Women brought king to Paris |
| **Constitution of 1791** | 1791 | Constitutional monarchy |

### Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Natural rights** | Liberty, property, security, resistance |
| **Equality** | Before the law |
| **Popular sovereignty** | "The nation" is sovereign |
| **Freedom of speech** | Expression protected |
| **Due process** | Legal protections |
| **Property** | Sacred right |

| Aspect | Details |

## üè≠ The Industrial Revolution

### Why Britain First?

| Factor | Advantage |
|--------|-----------|
| **Resources** | Coal, iron ore abundant |
| **Geography** | Rivers, harbors, island |
| **Capital** | Banking, investment available |
| **Labor** | Enclosure freed workers |
| **Government** | Stable; protected property |
| **Empire** | Markets and raw materials |
| **Culture** | Innovation valued |

### Agricultural Revolution (Precursor)

| Innovation | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| **Enclosure** | Consolidated land; displaced peasants |
| **Crop rotation** | Four-field system; higher yields |
| **New tools** | Seed drill (Jethro Tull) |
| **Selective breeding** | Better livestock |
| **Result** | Food surplus; population growth |

### Key Innovations

| Innovation | Inventor | Date | Impact |
|------------|----------|------|--------|
| **Flying shuttle** | Kay | 1733 | Faster weaving |
| **Spinning jenny** | Hargreaves | 1764 | Multiple threads |
| **Water frame** | Arkwright | 1769 | Water-powered spinning |
| **Steam engine** | Watt | 1769 | Power source |
| **Cotton gin** | Whitney | 1793 | Cotton processing |
| **Power loom** | Cartwright | 1785 | Mechanized weaving |
| **Steam locomotive** | Stephenson | 1814 | Rail transport |

### The Factory System

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Centralized** | Workers in one location |
| **Powered** | Water, then steam |
| **Division of labor** | Specialized tasks |
| **Clock discipline** | Timed work |
| **Supervision** | Managed workers |
| **Wage labor** | Paid by time/piece |

### Social Effects

| Change | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Urbanization** | Cities grew rapidly |
| **Class structure** | Industrial middle class; working class |
| **Family** | Changed roles; women, children in factories |
| **Living conditions** | Overcrowded; unsanitary |
| **Working conditions** | Long hours; dangerous; child labor |
| **Environment** | Pollution |

### Spread of Industrialization

| Country | When | Features |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Britain** | 1760s+ | First; textiles, iron |
| **Belgium** | 1820s | Continental leader |
| **France** | 1830s | Slower; luxury goods |
| **Germany** | 1840s | Steel, chemicals later |
| **United States** | 1840s | Rapid after Civil War |
| **Japan** | 1870s | Meiji era |
| **Russia** | 1890s | State-led |

### Comparing Revolutions

| Theme | Examples |

| **Violence** | Varied widely |
| **Outcomes** | Often limited; elites benefited |
| **Global connections** | Revolutions influenced each other |
