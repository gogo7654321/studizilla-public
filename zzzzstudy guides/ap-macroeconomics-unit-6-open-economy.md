## ğŸ“ FRQ Practice: Unit 6

### Question 1: Exchange Rate Determination

**The US Federal Reserve raises interest rates while the European Central Bank holds rates constant.**

**(a)** Using a correctly labeled graph of the dollar-euro foreign exchange market, show the effect on the exchange rate.

**(b)** Did the dollar appreciate or depreciate? Explain.

**(c)** What happens to US net exports? Explain.

**(d)** How does this affect US aggregate demand?

### Model Answer 1:

**(a)** FOREX Graph:
- Y-axis: Exchange rate (â‚¬/$)
- X-axis: Quantity of dollars
- Demand for dollars shifts RIGHT (European investors want US assets)
- Equilibrium exchange rate RISES (more euros per dollar)

**(b)** The dollar **APPRECIATED**:
- Higher US interest rates attract foreign capital
- Increased demand for dollars to invest in US assets
- Dollar becomes more valuable relative to euro
- Now each dollar buys MORE euros than before

**(c)** US net exports **DECREASE**:
- Stronger dollar makes US goods MORE expensive for Europeans
- US exports fall
- Stronger dollar makes European goods CHEAPER for Americans
- US imports rise
- NX = X - M decreases

**(d)** Effect on AD:
- Net exports is component of AD (AD = C + I + G + NX)
- Lower NX shifts AD LEFT
- This PARTIALLY OFFSETS the direct effect of lower investment from higher rates
- Overall AD effect is reduced

### Question 2: Balance of Payments

**Country X has a current account deficit of $50 billion.**

**(a)** What must be true about Country X's financial account? Explain.

**(b)** Is Country X a net borrower or net lender? Explain.

**(c)** What happens to Country X's currency if foreign investors become less willing to invest there?

### Model Answer 2:

**(a)** Country X must have a **financial account SURPLUS of $50 billion**.
- The balance of payments must sum to zero
- Current account + Financial account = 0
- If CA = -$50B, then FA = +$50B
- This means more capital is flowing INTO Country X than out

**(b)** Country X is a **net BORROWER** from the rest of the world:
- Current account deficit means spending more on foreign goods than earning from exports
- To finance this, Country X must attract foreign investment
- Foreigners are essentially lending to Country X
- Country X is living beyond its means (consuming more than producing)

**(c)** Country X's currency will **DEPRECIATE**:
- Less foreign investment means less demand for Country X's currency
- Demand curve for currency shifts LEFT
- Exchange rate falls (currency worth less)
- This makes exports cheaper, potentially improving trade balance

### Question 3: Policy Coordination

**A country is experiencing a recession with high unemployment and a trade deficit.**

**(a)** If the central bank uses expansionary monetary policy, what happens to the exchange rate and trade balance?

**(b)** If the government uses expansionary fiscal policy, what happens to the exchange rate and trade balance?

**(c)** Which policy better addresses BOTH problems? Explain.

### Model Answer 3:

**(a)** Expansionary Monetary Policy:
- Interest rates FALL
- Capital outflow increases
- Currency DEPRECIATES
- Exports become cheaper, imports more expensive
- Trade balance IMPROVES (deficit decreases)
- **Helps BOTH problems!**

**(b)** Expansionary Fiscal Policy:
- Government borrowing increases interest rates
- Capital inflow increases
- Currency APPRECIATES
- Exports become more expensive, imports cheaper
- Trade balance WORSENS (deficit increases)
- **Helps recession but WORSENS trade deficit**

**(c)** **Monetary policy** better addresses both problems:
- Lower rates stimulate domestic spending (fight recession)
- Currency depreciation boosts net exports (improves trade balance)
- Effects reinforce each other
- Fiscal policy would fight recession but worsen trade deficit through appreciation
