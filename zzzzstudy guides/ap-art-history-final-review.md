## üìù Exam Format and Strategies

### Exam Structure

```
SECTION I: MULTIPLE CHOICE (80 mins)
- 80 questions
- 50% of score
- Image-based
- Sets of questions per image(s)

SECTION II: FREE RESPONSE (120 mins)
- 6 questions total
- 50% of score

Question Types:
1. Short Answer (2 questions, 15 mins each)
   - Visual/contextual analysis
   - 30 minutes total

2. Long Essay (2 questions, 30 mins each)
   - Comparison
   - Attribution
   - 60 minutes total

3. Long Essay with Unknown (2 questions, 30 mins each)
   - Work not on image set
   - 60 minutes total
```

### Visual Analysis Skills

```
DESCRIBING ART:

FORMAL ELEMENTS:
- Line (quality, direction)
- Shape (geometric, organic)
- Color (hue, value, intensity)
- Texture (actual, implied)
- Space (positive, negative)
- Scale (size, proportion)

PRINCIPLES:
- Balance (symmetry, asymmetry)
- Emphasis (focal point)
- Movement (visual flow)
- Pattern (repetition)
- Unity (coherence)

VOCABULARY:
Use specific terms:
- "Contrapposto" not "standing pose"
- "Tenebrism" not "dark and light"
- "Pietra serena" not "grey stone"
```

### Contextual Analysis

```
CONSIDER:

CREATION:
- Who made it?
- When and where?
- What materials/techniques?
- For whom?

FUNCTION:
- Original purpose
- How was it used?
- Where was it displayed?
- Has function changed?

MEANING:
- What does it represent?
- Symbolism and iconography
- Cultural significance
- Audience interpretation

CONNECTIONS:
- Art historical precedents
- Contemporary context
- Influence on later art
- Cross-cultural connections
```

### Comparison Strategies

```
EFFECTIVE COMPARISONS:

STRUCTURE:
1. Identify works clearly
2. State thesis (argument)
3. Compare point by point
4. Analyze significance

AVOID:
- Listing features separately
- "Work A has X, Work B has Y"
- Without connecting analysis

INSTEAD:
- Integrate comparison
- "While both works show X, 
   A emphasizes Y whereas B..."
- Explain WHY differences matter

TYPES OF COMPARISON:
- Form (visual elements)
- Function (purpose/use)
- Content (subject/meaning)
- Context (historical/cultural)
```
