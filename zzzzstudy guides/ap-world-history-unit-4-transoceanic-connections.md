## ÔøΩüìã Unit Overview

The period from 1450-1750 witnessed the most significant transformation in global connections since the original human migrations. European explorers crossed the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, linking the Eastern and Western Hemispheres for the first time since the land bridge closed. This created truly global networks of trade, migration, and cultural exchange! ‚õµ

## üîÑ The Columbian Exchange

### Definition

The Columbian Exchange refers to the transfer of plants, animals, diseases, people, and ideas between the Eastern and Western Hemispheres after 1492.

### Biological Transfers: Americas to Old World

| Category | Items | Impact |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Food crops** | Potatoes, corn, tomatoes, squash | Population growth in Europe, Africa, Asia |
| **Other crops** | Tobacco, cacao, vanilla, peanuts | New products, trade |
| **Animals** | Turkeys, llamas (limited) | Minor impact |
| **Disease** | Syphilis (debated) | Spread in Europe |

### Biological Transfers: Old World to Americas

| Category | Items | Impact |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Food crops** | Wheat, rice, barley, sugarcane | New agriculture |
| **Animals** | Horses, cattle, pigs, sheep, chickens | Transformed Americas |
| **Diseases** | Smallpox, measles, typhus, influenza | Demographic catastrophe |
| **Technology** | Iron, wheel, firearms | New capabilities |

### Impact of New World Crops

| Crop | Where | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Potato** | Europe, especially Ireland | Population boom; calorie-dense |
| **Corn (maize)** | Africa, China | Adaptable; population growth |
| **Tomato** | Italy, Mediterranean | Transformed cuisine (eventually) |
| **Sweet potato** | China | New food source |
| **Manioc/cassava** | Africa | Tropical staple |

### Impact of Old World Animals

| Animal | Impact on Americas |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Horses** | Transformed Plains Indians; warfare |
| **Cattle** | Ranching economy; meat, leather |
| **Pigs** | Quickly multiplied; food source |
| **Sheep** | Wool production |
| **Chickens** | Common food source |

### The Demographic Catastrophe

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Population decline** | 50-90% of indigenous Americans |
| **Main cause** | Disease (smallpox, measles, typhus) |
| **Why so deadly** | No immunity; virgin soil epidemics |
| **Timeline** | 1492-1600s |
| **Hispaniola** | 1 million to hundreds in 50 years |
| **Mexico** | 20 million to 2 million |
| **Consequences** | Labor shortage ‚Üí African slavery |
