## üìà Human Population Growth

### Historical Growth

| Period | Population | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **10,000 BCE** | ~5 million | Hunter-gatherers |
| **1 CE** | ~300 million | Agricultural societies |
| **1800** | ~1 billion | Pre-industrial |
| **1960** | ~3 billion | Industrial growth |
| **2000** | ~6 billion | Modern era |
| **2024** | ~8 billion | Current |

### Growth Rate

| Formula | |
|---------|--|
| **Growth rate (%)** | = (CBR - CDR) / 10 |
| **Or** | = (Births - Deaths) / Population √ó 100 |

### Doubling Time

| Rule of 70 | |
|------------|--|
| **Doubling time** | = 70 / Growth rate (%) |
| **Example** | 2% growth = 35 years to double |

## üìä Demographic Indicators

### Birth Rate (Crude Birth Rate - CBR)

| Formula | CBR = (Births / Population) √ó 1,000 |
|---------|-------------------------------------|
| **Units** | Births per 1,000 people per year |

### Death Rate (Crude Death Rate - CDR)

| Formula | CDR = (Deaths / Population) √ó 1,000 |
|---------|-------------------------------------|
| **Units** | Deaths per 1,000 people per year |

### Natural Increase

| Formula | NIR = CBR - CDR |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Positive** | Population growing |
| **Negative** | Population declining |
| **Zero** | Stable population |

### Total Fertility Rate (TFR)

| Definition | Average children per woman |
|------------|---------------------------|
| **Replacement level** | 2.1 children |
| **Above 2.1** | Population grows |
| **Below 2.1** | Population declines (long-term) |

### Infant Mortality Rate

| Definition | Deaths under age 1 per 1,000 live births |
|------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Indicator of** | Healthcare quality |
| **High IMR** | Developing countries |
| **Low IMR** | Developed countries |

## üîÑ Demographic Transition Model

### Overview

| Model Shows | Change from high birth/death rates to low rates |
|-------------|------------------------------------------------|

### Stage 1: Pre-Industrial

| Characteristic | Value |
|----------------|-------|
| **Birth rate** | High |
| **Death rate** | High |
| **Population** | Stable (low) |
| **Growth rate** | Very low |
| **Example** | No modern countries |

### Stage 2: Transitional (Early Industrial)

| Characteristic | Value |
|----------------|-------|
| **Birth rate** | High |
| **Death rate** | Declining |
| **Population** | Rapidly growing |
| **Growth rate** | High |
| **Example** | Some developing countries |

### Stage 3: Industrial (Late Transitional)

| Characteristic | Value |
|----------------|-------|
| **Birth rate** | Declining |
| **Death rate** | Low |
| **Population** | Still growing |
| **Growth rate** | Decreasing |
| **Example** | Brazil, India |

### Stage 4: Post-Industrial

| Characteristic | Value |
|----------------|-------|
| **Birth rate** | Low |
| **Death rate** | Low |
| **Population** | Stable |
| **Growth rate** | Very low or zero |
| **Example** | USA, UK, France |

### Stage 5: Declining (Proposed)

| Characteristic | Value |
|----------------|-------|
| **Birth rate** | Very low |
| **Death rate** | Low |
| **Population** | Declining |
| **Growth rate** | Negative |
| **Example** | Japan, Germany, Italy |

| **2** | High | Falling | High |

## üìà Population Momentum

### Definition

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Population momentum** | Growth continues even after TFR drops |
| **Why** | Large cohort of young people not yet reproducing |
| **Duration** | Can last decades |

### Example

| Scenario | |
|----------|--|
| **If TFR drops to 2.1** | Population still grows |
| **Because** | Many young people will have children |
| **Until** | Age structure becomes stable |

## üìù Free Response Question Practice

### FRQ Practice 1: Population Calculations

**Scenario:** A country has the following data:
- Population: 50 million
- Births: 1.5 million/year
- Deaths: 0.5 million/year

**(a)** Calculate the crude birth rate, crude death rate, and natural increase rate. (4 points)

**(b)** Calculate the doubling time for this population. (2 points)

**(c)** Identify the demographic transition stage and provide evidence. (2 points)

**(d)** Predict how this country's age structure diagram would change over the next 50 years if it progresses through the demographic transition. (2 points)

### Sample Answer Rubric:

| Part | Key Points |
|------|------------|
| **(a)** | CBR = (1.5M/50M) √ó 1000 = **30**; CDR = (0.5M/50M) √ó 1000 = **10**; NIR = 30-10 = **20 per 1000** = 2% |
| **(b)** | Doubling time = 70/2 = **35 years** |
| **(c)** | Stage 2 (transitional); high birth rate, declining death rate |
| **(d)** | Base would narrow as birth rate falls; bulge would move upward as population ages |

### FRQ Practice 2: Demographic Transition

**Scenario:** Country X has transitioned from Stage 2 to Stage 4 over 60 years.

**(a)** Describe the changes in birth rate and death rate that occurred. (3 points)

**(b)** Explain two factors that caused the death rate to decline first. (2 points)

**(c)** Explain two factors that caused the birth rate to decline later. (2 points)

**(d)** Predict challenges this country will face in Stage 4 or 5. (2 points)

### FRQ Practice 3: Population and Environment

**Scenario:** Country A has 10 million people with an ecological footprint of 8 gha/person. Country B has 100 million people with a footprint of 1 gha/person.

**(a)** Calculate the total ecological impact of each country. Which has greater impact? (3 points)

**(b)** Apply the IPAT equation to explain the difference. (3 points)

**(c)** Propose two strategies for Country A to reduce its ecological footprint. (2 points)

**(d)** As Country B develops economically, predict what will happen to its footprint. (2 points)
