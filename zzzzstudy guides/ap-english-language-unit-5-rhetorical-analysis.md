## üîç What Is Rhetorical Analysis?

### Definition

| Rhetorical Analysis | Examining HOW a text works to achieve its purpose |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------|

### What Rhetorical Analysis Is

| Yes | No |
|-----|-----|
| HOW the argument works | WHAT the argument says |
| Analyzing strategies | Summarizing content |
| Explaining effects | Listing devices |
| Connecting to purpose | Personal opinion |
| Examining choices | Evaluating truth |

### The Rhetorical Analysis Question

| Central Question | Why did the writer make THIS choice for THIS audience in THIS situation? |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|

### Analysis vs. Summary

| Summary | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| WHAT the text says | HOW the text works |
| Retelling content | Explaining strategies |
| "The author argues that..." | "The author uses X to create Y effect..." |
| Facts about text | Interpretation of choices |

## üé≠ Identifying Rhetorical Strategies

### Aristotle's Appeals

| Appeal | Definition | How to Identify | Example Analysis |
|--------|------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Ethos** | Credibility, character | Credentials, tone, fairness | "By citing her medical training, the author establishes authority" |
| **Pathos** | Emotion | Stories, vivid imagery, loaded words | "The graphic description of suffering evokes pity" |
| **Logos** | Logic, reasoning | Statistics, evidence, structure | "The causal chain demonstrates inevitable consequences" |
| **Kairos** | Timeliness | References to moment, urgency | "Opening with 'now more than ever' creates urgency" |

### Ethos Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Credentials** | Cites expertise | "As a 20-year educator..." |
| **Common ground** | Shared values | "Like you, I believe in..." |
| **Fairness** | Acknowledges opposition | "Critics make valid points..." |
| **Tone** | Professional manner | Formal diction, balanced syntax |
| **Personal experience** | Lived authority | "Having survived this myself..." |

### Pathos Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Anecdote** | Personal story | "Maria was only 12 when..." |
| **Vivid imagery** | Sensory details | "The acrid smell of smoke..." |
| **Loaded diction** | Emotional words | "Innocent victims" vs "casualties" |
| **Rhetorical questions** | Engage feelings | "How would you feel if...?" |
| **Direct address** | Personal connection | "You can make a difference" |

### Logos Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Statistics** | Numerical proof | "80% of studies confirm..." |
| **Examples** | Concrete instances | "In California, the policy..." |
| **Cause-effect** | Logical chain | "Poverty leads to X, which causes Y" |
| **Comparison** | Analogy, precedent | "Like the New Deal, this policy..." |
| **Expert testimony** | Authority support | "According to Dr. Smith..." |

## üìù The Rhetorical Analysis Essay

### Essay Structure

| Section | Content | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Introduction** | Context + thesis | Establish focus |
| **Body 1** | Strategy + evidence + analysis | Develop first point |
| **Body 2** | Strategy + evidence + analysis | Develop second point |
| **Body 3** | Strategy + evidence + analysis | Develop third point |
| **Conclusion** | Synthesis + significance | Show broader meaning |

### The Thesis Statement

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Identifies writer** | Who is speaking |
| **States purpose** | What they want to achieve |
| **Names strategies** | What choices you'll analyze |
| **Implies effect** | How strategies work |

### Thesis Templates

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Writer uses X, Y, Z to achieve purpose** | "King uses biblical allusion, anaphora, and direct address to inspire hope in his audience" |
| **Through X and Y, writer accomplishes Z** | "Through emotional anecdotes and statistical evidence, the author convinces readers of climate urgency" |
| **By employing X, writer creates Y effect** | "By employing ironic understatement and satirical comparisons, Swift criticizes English exploitation" |

### Weak vs. Strong Thesis

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "The author uses many rhetorical strategies" | "The author builds credibility through personal anecdote while using stark statistics to create urgency" |
| "King's speech is very persuasive" | "King's strategic repetition of 'I have a dream' transforms his specific critique into a universal vision" |
| "The author uses ethos, pathos, and logos" | "The author balances clinical statistics with humanizing stories to persuade skeptical readers" |

## üìö Sample Rhetorical Analysis Paragraphs

### Model Body Paragraph 1: Analyzing Ethos

**Topic Sentence:** King establishes credibility by positioning himself within a tradition of moral authority that his audience cannot easily dismiss.

**Evidence:** Early in the letter, King compares himself to the Apostle Paul, writing that "just as Paul left his village of Tarsus and carried the gospel of Jesus Christ," he too must respond to calls for aid beyond his hometown.

**Analysis:** This biblical allusion serves multiple functions. For his audience of Christian clergymen, the comparison places King's activism within a framework they must respect‚ÄîChristian missionary work. Moreover, by invoking Paul, King transforms his "outsider" status from a liability into a spiritual calling, suggesting that his critics' objection to outside interference fails to understand the universal nature of moral duty.

### Model Body Paragraph 2: Analyzing Pathos

**Topic Sentence:** Through a masterful use of accumulated detail, King makes abstract injustice viscerally present to readers who have never experienced it.

**Evidence:** King writes, "when you have seen vicious mobs lynch your mothers and fathers at will and drown your sisters and brothers at whim; when you have seen hate-filled policemen curse, kick, and even kill your black brothers and sisters..."

**Analysis:** The anaphora of "when you have seen" forces readers to adopt the witnessing position, making them imaginative participants in these horrors. The progression from family members‚Äî"mothers and fathers," "sisters and brothers"‚Äîinvokes the universal bonds that cross racial lines, compelling white readers to recognize the humanity of those suffering. The verbs escalate from "curse" to "kick" to "kill," building to an emotional crescendo that makes continued patience seem not virtuous but complicit.

### Model Body Paragraph 3: Analyzing Logos

**Topic Sentence:** King systematically dismantles his critics' arguments through a logical structure that anticipates and addresses each objection.

**Evidence:** The letter proceeds through a series of questions King attributes to his critics: "Why direct action? Why sit-ins? Why not negotiation?" Each question is posed and then methodically answered.

**Analysis:** This question-and-answer structure (known as hypophora) accomplishes several rhetorical goals simultaneously. It demonstrates King's thorough understanding of opposing viewpoints, strengthening his ethos as a fair-minded interlocutor. It allows him to control the terms of debate by selecting which objections to address. Most importantly, it creates a sense of logical inevitability‚Äîonce each objection is refuted, readers are left with no rational basis for opposition.
