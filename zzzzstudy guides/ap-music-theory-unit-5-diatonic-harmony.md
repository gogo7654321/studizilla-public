## üîÄ 5.4 Common Chord Progressions

### Basic Progression Patterns

```
COMMON PROGRESSIONS:

I - IV - V - I
(Tonic - Subdominant - Dominant - Tonic)
Most fundamental progression

I - V - vi - IV
(The "pop" progression)
Countless pop songs use this

ii - V - I
(Jazz foundation)
Most important jazz progression

I - vi - IV - V
(50s doo-wop)
Classic sound

vi - IV - I - V
(Emo/sad pop)
Starts on minor
```

### Progression Logic

```
FUNCTIONAL HARMONY:

TONIC (T): Rest, home
Chords: I, vi, iii

SUBDOMINANT (S): Away from home
Chords: IV, ii

DOMINANT (D): Tension, leads home
Chords: V, vii¬∞

TYPICAL FLOW:
T ‚Üí S ‚Üí D ‚Üí T
(Home ‚Üí Away ‚Üí Tension ‚Üí Resolution)

VALID PROGRESSIONS:
T ‚Üí D ‚Üí T (skip subdominant)
T ‚Üí S ‚Üí T (skip dominant, weaker)
T ‚Üí S ‚Üí D ‚Üí T (complete cycle)
```

### Root Movement

```
ROOT MOTION TENDENCIES:

STRONG PROGRESSIONS:
- Down a 5th (or up 4th): V ‚Üí I, ii ‚Üí V
- Down a 3rd: I ‚Üí vi, IV ‚Üí ii

WEAK/UNUSUAL:
- Up a 5th (or down 4th): I ‚Üí V
- Up a 2nd: IV ‚Üí V (common anyway)

CIRCLE OF FIFTHS PROGRESSION:
I ‚Üí IV ‚Üí vii¬∞ ‚Üí iii ‚Üí vi ‚Üí ii ‚Üí V ‚Üí I
(All descending 5ths)
```

## üé≠ 5.5 Cadences

### Authentic Cadences

```
PERFECT AUTHENTIC CADENCE (PAC):
V ‚Üí I (or V7 ‚Üí I)
- Both chords in root position
- Soprano ends on tonic (scale degree 1)
- Strongest sense of finality

IMPERFECT AUTHENTIC CADENCE (IAC):
V ‚Üí I
- One or both inverted, OR
- Soprano ends on 3rd or 5th
- Less conclusive

Examples (C major):
PAC: G-B-D ‚Üí C-E-G (soprano G‚ÜíC)
IAC: G-B-D ‚Üí E-G-C (soprano B‚ÜíE)
```

### Half Cadence

```
HALF CADENCE (HC):
Any chord ‚Üí V
(Ends on dominant)

Like a musical "comma"
Creates expectation

Common approach chords:
I ‚Üí V
ii ‚Üí V
IV ‚Üí V
vi ‚Üí V

PHRYGIAN HALF CADENCE:
iv6 ‚Üí V in minor
(‚ô≠6 in bass moves to 5)
Spanish/dramatic sound
```

### Plagal and Deceptive Cadences

```
PLAGAL CADENCE:
IV ‚Üí I
"Amen" cadence
Softer conclusion than authentic

DECEPTIVE CADENCE (DC):
V ‚Üí vi (instead of I)
"Deceptive" because we expect I
Prolongs the phrase
Creates surprise

In minor: V ‚Üí VI
(G major ‚Üí F major in A minor)
```

### Cadence Summary

```
CADENCE TYPES:

Name      Formula    Effect
PAC       V‚ÜíI        Full stop
IAC       V‚ÜíI        Softer stop
HC        ?‚ÜíV        Comma, pause
Plagal    IV‚ÜíI       Amen ending
Deceptive V‚Üívi       Surprise

IDENTIFYING CADENCES:
1. Find phrase endings
2. Check last two chords
3. Check bass motion
4. Check soprano line

## üéµ 5.6 Voice Leading Basics

### Four-Part Writing

```
SATB VOICING:
Soprano (S): Highest voice
Alto (A): Second highest  
Tenor (T): Second lowest
Bass (B): Lowest voice

RANGES:
Soprano: C4 - G5
Alto: G3 - D5
Tenor: C3 - G4
Bass: E2 - C4

SPACING:
S-A: No more than octave
A-T: No more than octave
T-B: Can be larger
No voice crossing!
```

### Voice Leading Principles

```
SMOOTH VOICE LEADING:

1. COMMON TONES
   Keep notes shared between chords
   
2. STEPWISE MOTION
   Move other voices by step
   
3. CONTRARY MOTION
   Opposite direction to bass
   
4. AVOID LEAPS
   Large leaps less smooth
   (Leaps of 4th or less preferred)

EXAMPLE: C ‚Üí F
C-E-G-C ‚Üí C-F-A-C
C stays (common tone)
E moves to F (step)
G moves to A (step)
C stays (common tone)
```

### Voice Leading Errors

```
ERRORS TO AVOID:

PARALLEL 5THS:
Two voices moving in P5 ‚Üí P5
C-G ‚Üí D-A (both move up step)
FORBIDDEN in common practice

PARALLEL OCTAVES:
Two voices moving in P8 ‚Üí P8
C-C ‚Üí D-D (both voices same motion)
FORBIDDEN

DIRECT/HIDDEN 5THS/8VES:
Similar motion into P5 or P8
Outer voices arriving together
AVOID between soprano and bass

VOICE CROSSING:
Alto below tenor, etc.
AVOID
```

## ‚ö° 5.7 Dominant Function Chords

### The Dominant Seventh

```
V7 (Dominant Seventh):
Most important seventh chord!

STRUCTURE:
Major triad + minor 7th
Contains tritone (4 ‚Üí 7)

RESOLUTION:
7th (4) ‚Üí 3 (step down)
Leading tone (7) ‚Üí 1 (step up)
5 ‚Üí 1 (bass)

C MAJOR V7 ‚Üí I:
G-B-D-F ‚Üí C-C-E-G
F resolves down to E
B resolves up to C
```

## üìä 5.8 Harmonic Analysis

### Roman Numeral Analysis

```
ANALYSIS STEPS:

1. IDENTIFY KEY
   Look at key signature
   Check for accidentals
   
2. IDENTIFY BASS NOTE
   Determine scale degree
   
3. IDENTIFY CHORD QUALITY
   Major, minor, diminished?
   
4. DETERMINE INVERSION
   What's in the bass?
   
5. WRITE ROMAN NUMERAL
   With inversion figures

EXAMPLE (C major):
Bass: E, Chord: E-G-C
E = scale degree 3
Chord = C major (root C)
Inversion = 1st (3rd in bass)
Symbol: I6
```

### Non-Chord Tones (Preview)

```
EMBELLISHING TONES:

PASSING TONE (PT):
Fills gap by step
C ‚Üí D ‚Üí E (D is PT)

NEIGHBOR TONE (NT):
Step away and back
C ‚Üí D ‚Üí C (D is NT)

SUSPENSION (SUS):
Held over, resolves down
Tied note that resolves

ANTICIPATION (ANT):
Arrives early
Note of next chord
