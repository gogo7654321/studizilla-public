## üåç Map Projections

### The Projection Problem

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Challenge** | Can't flatten a sphere perfectly |
| **Distortion** | Shape, area, distance, or direction |
| **Trade-offs** | Preserve some properties; distort others |

### Major Projections

| Projection | Preserves | Distorts | Best Use |
|------------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Mercator** | Shape; direction | Area (polar enlargement) | Navigation |
| **Peters** | Area | Shape | Comparing sizes |
| **Robinson** | Balance | All slightly | General purpose |
| **Goode's** | Area | Shape (interrupted) | Thematic maps |
| **Azimuthal** | Direction from center | Shape at edges | Polar; airline routes |
| **Conic** | Area in mid-latitudes | Edges | Regional maps |

### Mercator Issues

| Distortion | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **Greenland** | Appears as large as Africa |
| **Africa** | 14x larger than Greenland actually |
| **Antarctica** | Stretched infinitely at poles |
| **Eurocentrism** | Europe appears central |

## üåê Map Projections Comparison

### Detailed Projection Analysis

| Projection | Type | Preserves | Distorts | Best Uses | Limitations |
|------------|------|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Mercator (1569)** | Cylindrical | Shape, angles | Area (extreme at poles) | Navigation, web maps | Greenland appears huge |
| **Peters (1974)** | Cylindrical | Area | Shape (stretched) | Comparing country sizes | Continents look distorted |
| **Robinson (1963)** | Pseudo-cylindrical | Balance | All slightly | General reference | Not equal-area or conformal |
| **Winkel Tripel** | Modified azimuthal | Balance | All slightly | National Geographic standard | Compromise projection |
| **Goode's Homolosine** | Interrupted | Area | Continuity | Thematic world maps | Ocean interruptions |
| **Mollweide** | Pseudo-cylindrical | Area | Shape at edges | Population distribution | Poles stretched |
| **Lambert Conformal Conic** | Conic | Shape, direction | Area at edges | Aeronautical charts | Limited to mid-latitudes |
| **Albers Equal Area Conic** | Conic | Area | Shape slightly | USGS maps | Best for east-west regions |
| **Azimuthal Equidistant** | Azimuthal | Distance from center | Shape, area at edges | UN flag, airline routes | Only accurate from center |
| **Stereographic** | Azimuthal | Shape locally | Area | Polar regions, star charts | Infinite at opposite pole |

### Why Projections Matter

| Issue | Geographic Impact | Example |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| **Size distortion** | Political perceptions | Mercator makes Global North appear dominant |
| **Shape distortion** | Navigation errors | Flat map great circle routes appear curved |
| **Direction distortion** | Compass inaccuracy | Some projections not suitable for navigation |
| **Cultural bias** | Euro-centric worldviews | Europe centered, larger; Africa minimized |

## üåç Geographic Models and Theories

### Key Geographic Models

| Model | Creator | Year | Concept | Application |
|-------|---------|------|---------|-------------|

| **Von Th√ºnen Model** | Von Th√ºnen | 1826 | Agricultural land use zones | Rural land use |
| **Heartland Theory** | Mackinder | 1904 | Control of Eurasia = world power | Geopolitics |

### Gravity Model Details

| Variable | Effect | Example |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Population of Place 1** | Larger = more interaction | NYC attracts more commuters than small town |
| **Population of Place 2** | Larger = more interaction | Shopping center in large city draws more |
| **Distance** | Greater = less interaction | Cross-country migration less than regional |
| **Formula** | Interaction = (Pop1 √ó Pop2) / Distance¬≤ | Predicts trade flows, phone calls, trips |

### Tobler's First Law of Geography

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Statement** | "Everything is related to everything else, but near things are more related than distant things" |
| **Significance** | Foundation of spatial analysis |
| **Applications** | Spatial autocorrelation, geostatistics |
| **Exceptions** | Modern technology reduces distance friction |
