### Problem 2: Energy of Transition
Calculate the energy of light emitted when an electron in hydrogen falls from n=4 to n=2.

**Solution:**
$$\Delta E = -2.18 \times 10^{-18}\left(\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{16}\right) = -2.18 \times 10^{-18}(0.1875)$$
$$= -4.09 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$$

(Negative means energy is emitted)

### Problem 3: Wavelength Calculation

**Solution:**
$$\lambda = \frac{hc}{E} = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{4.09 \times 10^{-19}} = 4.86 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m} = 486 \text{ nm}$$

(This is blue-green visible light - part of the Balmer series!)
