## üêõ Pest Control

### Types of Pesticides

| Type | Target |
|------|--------|
| **Insecticides** | Insects |
| **Herbicides** | Weeds |
| **Fungicides** | Fungi |
| **Rodenticides** | Rodents |

### Pesticide Problems

| Problem | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Resistance** | Pests evolve resistance |
| **Biomagnification** | Concentrates in food chain |
| **Non-target species** | Kills beneficial organisms |
| **Persistence** | Some don't break down |
| **Human health** | Exposure risks |

### Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **1. Prevention** | Crop rotation, resistant varieties |
| **2. Monitoring** | Scout for pest levels |
| **3. Threshold** | Only act if damage significant |
| **4. Control** | Biological, then chemical if needed |

### Biological Control

| Method | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Predators** | Ladybugs eat aphids |
| **Parasites** | Wasps parasitize pests |
| **Pathogens** | Bt bacteria kills caterpillars |

## üêü Fishing

### Overfishing

| Problem | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Definition** | Harvesting faster than reproduction |
| **Result** | Population collapse |
| **Examples** | Cod, tuna, sharks |

### Maximum Sustainable Yield (MSY)

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Definition** | Largest catch that can be sustained |
| **Goal** | Harvest at rate that allows recovery |
| **Challenge** | Difficult to calculate accurately |

### Fishing Methods

| Method | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Trawling** | Bottom destruction, bycatch |
| **Longlining** | Seabird, turtle bycatch |
| **Drift nets** | Ghost nets, bycatch |
| **Purse seining** | Dolphin bycatch |

### Bycatch

| Definition | |
|------------|--|
| **Unintended catch** | Non-target species |
| **Problem** | Waste, kills endangered species |
| **Solutions** | Turtle excluder devices, better nets |

### Aquaculture

| Definition | Farming of fish and shellfish |
|------------|------------------------------|
| **Advantages** | Reduces pressure on wild stocks |
| **Disadvantages** | Pollution, disease, escapes |

## üìù Free Response Question Practice

### FRQ Practice 1: Agriculture and Environment

**Scenario:** A farmer is considering switching from conventional to organic farming.

**(a)** Compare conventional and organic farming in terms of pesticide use, fertilizers, and yields. (3 points)

**(b)** Describe two environmental benefits of organic farming. (2 points)

**(c)** Explain two challenges the farmer might face during the transition. (2 points)

**(d)** Describe how Integrated Pest Management could reduce environmental impacts while maintaining yields. (3 points)

### Sample Answer Rubric:

| Part | Key Points |
|------|------------|
| **(a)** | Conventional: synthetic pesticides/fertilizers, higher yields; Organic: natural inputs, lower yields |
| **(b)** | Reduced water pollution, better soil health, biodiversity support |
| **(c)** | Lower initial yields, learning curve, certification costs, pest pressure |
| **(d)** | Prevention first, monitoring, threshold-based action, biological controls before chemicals |

### FRQ Practice 2: Forestry Management

**Scenario:** A national forest is being managed for multiple uses including timber, recreation, and wildlife.

**(a)** Compare clear-cutting and selective cutting in terms of environmental impacts. (3 points)

**(b)** Describe how the National Forest Management Act affects timber harvesting. (2 points)

**(c)** Explain the concept of sustainable forestry and how FSC certification promotes it. (2 points)

**(d)** Propose a management plan that balances timber production with wildlife conservation. (3 points)

### FRQ Practice 3: Urban Development

**Scenario:** A city is experiencing rapid suburban growth.

**(a)** Define urban sprawl and describe three of its environmental impacts. (4 points)

**(b)** Explain the concept of the urban heat island effect. (2 points)

**(c)** Compare smart growth principles with typical suburban development. (2 points)

**(d)** Propose two policies that would reduce the environmental impacts of urbanization. (2 points)
