## ğŸ”„ Sub-Theme 4: Artistic Traditions and Concepts

### Common Themes

```
ACROSS INDIGENOUS AMERICAS:

TRANSFORMATION:
- Human to animal
- Spirit world access
- Masks and costumes
- Shamanic practice

ASTRONOMY:
- Architectural alignments
- Calendar systems
- Solstice markers
- Cosmic cycles

HIERARCHY:
- Royal/elite patronage
- Material value (gold, feathers)
- Scale indicates status
- Sacred spaces restricted

NATURE/CULTURE:
- Integration with landscape
- Sacred geography
- Animal imagery
- Agricultural cycles

CONTINUITY:
- Living traditions
- Colonialism survived
- Contemporary practice
- Cultural revitalization
```

### Materials and Techniques

```
SIGNIFICANT MATERIALS:

GOLD AND SILVER:
- Divine associations
- Sun/moon symbolism
- Melted by Spanish
- Power and prestige

FEATHERS:
- Quetzal especially valued
- Color symbolism
- Fragile (few survive)
- Elite only

JADE/GREENSTONE:
- More valuable than gold (Maya)
- Life and water
- Breath/spirit
- Difficult to work

TEXTILES:
- Labor-intensive
- Status indicator
- Pattern meanings
- Camelid fibers (Andes)

STONE:
- Monumental architecture
- Fitted without mortar (Inca)
- Carved stelae (Maya)
- Effigy mounds
```
