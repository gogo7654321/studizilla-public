## üìã 3.2 Individual Research Report

### IRR Requirements

```
INDIVIDUAL RESEARCH REPORT:

LENGTH: 1,200 words maximum
PURPOSE: Your individual research contribution

MUST INCLUDE:
- Clear research question
- Thesis/claim
- Evidence from sources
- Analysis and reasoning
- Synthesis of sources
- Proper citations

FORMAT:
- Academic style
- Proper citations
- Bibliography included
- Clean formatting
```

### IRR Structure

```
RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE:

INTRODUCTION (150-200 words):
- Context for topic
- Research question
- Your thesis/claim
- Preview of argument

BODY PARAGRAPHS (700-900 words):
- Multiple paragraphs
- Each with clear point
- Evidence + analysis
- Synthesis across sources

CONCLUSION (100-150 words):
- Summarize argument
- Implications
- Connection to larger issues

BIBLIOGRAPHY:
- All sources cited
- Proper format
- Not counted in word limit
```

### IRR Rubric Focus

```
SCORING CRITERIA:

UNDERSTAND & ANALYZE ARGUMENT (3 pts):
- Thesis is clear and arguable
- Argument is developed
- Analysis is insightful

EVALUATE SOURCES & EVIDENCE (3 pts):
- Sources are credible
- Evidence is relevant
- Limitations acknowledged

RESEARCH & COMPOSITION (2 pts):
- Well-organized
- Academic style
- Clear writing

UNDERSTAND & ANALYZE CONTEXT (2 pts):
- Topic situated in context
- Relevant background
- Complexity acknowledged
```

## üé¨ 3.3 Team Multimedia Presentation

### TMP Requirements

```
TEAM MULTIMEDIA PRESENTATION:

LENGTH: 8-10 minutes total
TEAM SIZE: 3-5 members typically
FORMAT: Multimedia required

MUST INCLUDE:
- All team members presenting
- Synthesized research findings
- Multiple perspectives
- Visual/media elements
- Smooth transitions
- Team cohesion

FOLLOWED BY:
- Oral defense (Q&A)
- Questions from teacher/panel
```

### Presentation Structure

```
TMP ORGANIZATION:

OPENING (1-2 minutes):
- Hook/attention getter
- Topic introduction
- Research question
- Thesis/focus preview
- Speaker 1

BODY (5-6 minutes):
- Multiple perspectives
- Evidence and analysis
- Visual supports
- All speakers contribute
- Clear transitions

CONCLUSION (1-2 minutes):
- Synthesis of findings
- Implications
- Final thoughts
- Speaker transition to Q&A

TIMING:
Divide equitably among speakers
Practice for smooth transitions
Build in transition time
```

### Multimedia Elements

```
EFFECTIVE MULTIMEDIA:

VISUAL AIDS:
- Slides (not text-heavy)
- Images and graphics
- Charts and graphs
- Video clips (brief)
- Infographics

DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Consistent formatting
- Readable fonts
- Limited text per slide
- High-quality images
- Color scheme

TECHNOLOGY OPTIONS:
- PowerPoint
- Google Slides
- Prezi
- Canva
- Video integration

AVOID:
- Reading from slides
- Too much text
- Poor quality images
- Distracting animations
- Technical difficulties
```

### Team Presentation Skills

```
PRESENTING AS A TEAM:

COORDINATION:
- Know your cues
- Smooth transitions
- Consistent style
- Mutual support

TRANSITIONS BETWEEN SPEAKERS:
"I've discussed X; now [Name] will address Y."
"Building on [Name]'s point, I'll explore..."
"As [Name] mentioned, this connects to..."

DURING OTHERS' SECTIONS:
- Stay engaged
- Show attention
- Don't distract
- Be ready for your turn

TEAM PRESENCE:
- Unified front
- Professional demeanor
- Support each other
- Handle problems gracefully
```

## üó£Ô∏è 3.4 Oral Defense

### Preparing for Questions

```
Q&A PREPARATION:

ANTICIPATE QUESTIONS:
- What might they ask?
- What's unclear?
- What's controversial?
- What needs more explanation?

PREPARE ANSWERS:
- Research beyond presentation
- Know your sources well
- Prepare concise responses
- Practice articulating

TEAM STRATEGY:
- Who answers what?
- How to redirect?
- Support each other
- Fill gaps

COMMON QUESTION TYPES:
- Clarification
- Extension
- Challenge
- Application
```

### Responding Effectively

```
ANSWERING Q&A:

STRUCTURE YOUR RESPONSE:
1. Acknowledge the question
2. Answer directly
3. Provide support/example
4. Connect to argument

USEFUL PHRASES:
"That's a great question..."
"To clarify..."
"Building on what we said..."
"The research suggests..."

HANDLING DIFFICULTY:
- If you don't know: "That's beyond our 
  research scope, but we could explore..."
- If it's challenging: Address honestly,
  acknowledge complexity
- If it's unclear: "Could you clarify...?"

TEAM SUPPORT:
"I'd like to add to what [Name] said..."
"To build on that point..."
```

### Defense Strategies

```
ORAL DEFENSE EXCELLENCE:

KNOW YOUR MATERIAL:
- Understand ALL sources
- Know team's full argument
- Prepared for any question

LISTEN CAREFULLY:
- Understand the question
- Don't interrupt
- Think before speaking

ANSWER COMPLETELY:
- Address the actual question
- Provide evidence
- Connect to argument

STAY COMPOSED:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Handle challenges gracefully
- Acknowledge limitations

TEAM AWARENESS:
- Share speaking time
- Support teammates
- Present unified front
```

## ‚öñÔ∏è 3.6 Managing Team Challenges

### Common Team Problems

```
CHALLENGE: UNEVEN CONTRIBUTION
SOLUTIONS:
- Clear task assignment
- Regular check-ins
- Document contributions
- Address early
- Communicate concerns

CHALLENGE: DISAGREEMENT
SOLUTIONS:
- Focus on ideas, not people
- Return to evidence
- Seek compromise
- Agree to disagree respectfully
- Find common ground

CHALLENGE: COMMUNICATION BREAKDOWN
SOLUTIONS:
- Establish clear channels
- Regular meetings
- Written agreements
- Check in more frequently
- Clarify misunderstandings

CHALLENGE: DIFFERENT QUALITY STANDARDS
SOLUTIONS:
- Agree on standards early
- Share examples
- Peer review within team
- Revise together
- Support improvement
```

### Conflict Resolution

```
ADDRESSING CONFLICT:

STEP 1: IDENTIFY
What exactly is the problem?
Who is affected?
What's the impact?

STEP 2: COMMUNICATE
Talk directly (not behind backs)
Use "I" statements
Focus on behavior, not person
Listen to understand

STEP 3: FIND SOLUTIONS
Brainstorm options
Evaluate together
Agree on action
Document decision

STEP 4: FOLLOW UP
Check if solution works
Adjust if needed
Move forward positively
```
