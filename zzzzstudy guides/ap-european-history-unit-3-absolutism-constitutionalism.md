## ÔøΩüìã Unit Overview

After the devastating Wars of Religion, European states faced a crucial question: How should political power be organized? Two competing answers emerged. Absolutism concentrated power in the hands of monarchs who claimed divine right. Constitutionalism limited royal power through representative assemblies and rule of law. This period shaped modern ideas about government! üëë

### Essential Questions

| Question | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| What is absolutism? | Unlimited royal power |
| What is constitutionalism? | Limited, law-based government |
| Why did France become absolutist? | Louis XIV; state-building |
| Why did England become constitutional? | Civil War; Glorious Revolution |
| How did states grow stronger? | Bureaucracy; armies; taxation |

### Key Themes

| Theme | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **State-building** | Centralization; bureaucracy |
| **Absolutism** | France; Prussia; Russia |

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|

| **Divine right** | Power comes from God |

|---------|-------------|

## üìä Comparing Political Systems

### Absolutism vs. Constitutionalism

| Feature | Absolutism | Constitutionalism |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| **Power** | Concentrated in monarch | Limited; shared |
| **Parliament** | Weak or absent | Powerful |
| **Law** | King makes law | King under law |
| **Rights** | Granted by king | Inherent |
| **Example** | France | England |

### Why Different Paths?

| Factor | Absolutism | Constitutionalism |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Nobility** | Weaker; co-opted | Stronger; resisted |
| **Parliament tradition** | Weak | Strong |
| **Geography** | Larger; land powers | Island; naval |
| **Religion** | Unity imposed | More tolerant |
| **Civil war outcome** | Monarchy won (France) | Parliament won (England) |
