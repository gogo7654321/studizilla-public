## ğŸ¨ Shaping

### Definition

Reinforcing successive approximations to desired behavior.

### Example: Teaching a Pigeon to Turn

1. Reinforce any movement to the left
2. Reinforce only larger movements left
3. Reinforce only quarter turns

## ğŸˆ The Bobo Doll Experiment

### Procedure

## âœï¸ Sample Free Response

**Question**: Explain how each of the following concepts applies to a student who studies hard for a test.

(a) Positive reinforcement
(b) Negative reinforcement
(c) Fixed interval schedule
(d) Observational learning

**Answer**:

(a) **Positive reinforcement**: When the student receives good grades or praise from parents and teachers after studying hard, this adds a pleasant consequence. The student is more likely to study hard in the future because the behavior was followed by something positive (praise, grades).

(b) **Negative reinforcement**: Studying hard might remove or avoid unpleasant stimuli. For example, studying removes anxiety about failing, or studying means parents stop nagging. The behavior increases because something unpleasant is removed or avoided.

(c) **Fixed interval schedule**: The student's studying might follow a fixed interval pattern around exam time. Tests occur at predictable intervals (e.g., every Friday), so the student studies more as the test approaches and less immediately after. This creates a "scalloped" pattern with increased effort near the scheduled reinforcement.

(d) **Observational learning**: The student may have learned study habits by watching successful peers, older siblings, or parents. By observing others who were rewarded for studying (got good grades, went to college), the student is motivated to imitate their study behavior. The student pays attention to study methods, remembers them, reproduces them, and is motivated by the observed success.
