## 1.1 Fluid Statics

### Density and Pressure

**Density:**
$$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

| Substance | Density (kg/m¬≥) |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Air | 1.29 |
| Water | 1000 |
| Mercury | 13,600 |
| Aluminum | 2,700 |
| Iron | 7,900 |
| Gold | 19,300 |

**Pressure:**
$$P = \frac{F}{A}$$

- **Units:** Pascal (Pa) = N/m¬≤
- **1 atm** = 101,325 Pa = 760 mmHg = 14.7 psi

### Pressure in Fluids

**Pressure at Depth:**
$$P = P_0 + \rho gh$$

Where:
- P‚ÇÄ = surface pressure
- œÅ = fluid density
- g = gravitational acceleration
- h = depth below surface

**Key Concepts:**
- Pressure acts equally in all directions at a point
- Pressure depends only on depth, not container shape
- Gauge pressure = P - P‚ÇÄ

### Pascal's Principle

> *Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished to every point.*

$$\frac{F_1}{A_1} = \frac{F_2}{A_2}$$

**Applications:**
- Hydraulic lifts
- Hydraulic brakes
- Hydraulic presses

**Mechanical Advantage:**
$$MA = \frac{F_2}{F_1} = \frac{A_2}{A_1}$$

### Buoyancy and Archimedes' Principle

**Buoyant Force:**
$$F_B = \rho_{fluid} \cdot V_{displaced} \cdot g$$

**Floating Objects:**
- Object floats when F_B = Weight
- Fraction submerged = œÅ_object / œÅ_fluid

**Apparent Weight:**
$$W_{apparent} = W_{actual} - F_B$$

| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| œÅ_object < œÅ_fluid | Floats |
| œÅ_object = œÅ_fluid | Neutral buoyancy |
| œÅ_object > œÅ_fluid | Sinks |

## 1.2 Fluid Dynamics

### Continuity Equation

**Conservation of Mass:**
$$A_1v_1 = A_2v_2$$

- Flow rate (Q) = Av = constant
- Smaller cross-section ‚Üí faster flow

### Bernoulli's Equation

$$P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 + \rho gh_1 = P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 + \rho gh_2$$

**Simplified (same height):**
$$P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 = P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2$$

**Applications:**
| Phenomenon | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| Airplane lift | Fast air above wing ‚Üí lower pressure |
| Venturi effect | Narrow section ‚Üí faster flow ‚Üí lower pressure |
| Spray atomizer | Fast air ‚Üí low pressure pulls up liquid |
| Curve balls | Spinning ball creates pressure difference |

### Torricelli's Theorem

For fluid leaving a tank opening:
$$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Where h = height of fluid above opening

## 5.1 Magnetic Fields

### Magnetic Field Properties

## 5.2 Magnetic Force on Moving Charges

$$\vec{F} = q\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$$

$$F = qvB\sin\theta$$

**Right-Hand Rule:**
1. Point fingers in direction of v
2. Curl toward B
3. Thumb points in direction of F (for + charge)

### Motion in Magnetic Fields

|-------------------|------|
| Parallel to B | Straight line (no force) |
| Perpendicular to B | Circle |

**Circular Motion:**
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

## 5.3 Magnetic Force on Current-Carrying Wire

$$\vec{F} = I\vec{L} \times \vec{B}$$

$$F = BIL\sin\theta$$

**Applications:**
- Electric motors
- Speakers
- Galvanometers

**Steps to Apply:**

### Types of Decay

### Decay Equations

**Alpha:**
$$^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + ^4_2He$$

## 7.6 Nuclear Reactions

### Fission

- Heavy nucleus splits into lighter nuclei
- Releases energy and neutrons
- Example: U-235 fission

$$^{235}_{92}U + ^1_0n \rightarrow ^{141}_{56}Ba + ^{92}_{36}Kr + 3^1_0n$$

### Fusion

- Light nuclei combine to form heavier nucleus
- Releases enormous energy
- Example: Hydrogen fusion in stars

$$^2_1H + ^3_1H \rightarrow ^4_2He + ^1_0n$$

### Conservation Laws

In all nuclear reactions:
- Mass-energy is conserved
- Charge is conserved
- Nucleon number is conserved
- Momentum is conserved

### Fluids
- ‚ùå Thinking pressure depends on container shape
- ‚úÖ Pressure depends only on depth

### Thermodynamics

- ‚ùå Confusing B field direction and force direction

- ‚ùå Using wrong formula for interference/diffraction
