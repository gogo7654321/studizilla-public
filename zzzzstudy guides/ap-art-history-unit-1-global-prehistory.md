### Apollo 11 Stones

```
APOLLO 11 STONES (NAMIBIA):

DISCOVERY: 1969 (during Apollo 11 mission)
DATE: ~25,500-25,300 BCE
MATERIAL: Stone slabs with charcoal

SIGNIFICANCE:
- Oldest known African art
- Shows animal figure (possibly feline)
- Deliberate artistic intent
- Evidence of symbolic thinking
- Links to later San rock art

ANALYSIS:
- Portable art (could be moved)
- Part of ritual practice?
- Shows early human cognition
- Cross-cultural connections
```

### Venus Figurines (Context)

```
PALEOLITHIC FIGURINES:

CHARACTERISTICS:
- Small, portable
- Exaggerated features
- Female figures common
- Found across Europe
- ~40,000-10,000 BCE

COMMON FEATURES:
- Large breasts
- Wide hips
- Prominent bellies
- Often faceless
- Small/absent limbs

EXAMPLES:
- Venus of Willendorf
- Venus of Hohle Fels
- Venus of Brassempouy

INTERPRETATIONS:
- Fertility symbols
- Mother goddess worship
- Self-representations
- Teaching aids
- Good luck charms
```

### Transition to Settled Life

```
NEOLITHIC REVOLUTION:

CHANGES:
- Agriculture developed
- Permanent settlements
- Animal domestication

```

```
- Connection to nature
```

### Mesoamerican Art

```
TLATILCO FIGURINES:

CULTURE: Tlatilco, Mexico
DATE: ~1200-900 BCE
MATERIAL: Terra cotta

CHARACTERISTICS:
- Small female figures
- Elaborate hairstyles
- Some with two faces

- Bone carved into dog shape
- ~14,000-7,000 BCE
```

## ğŸ” Analysis Framework

### Formal Analysis

```
ANALYZING PREHISTORIC ART:

FORM:
- What does it look like?
- Size and scale
- Materials used
- Techniques visible
- Condition today

FUNCTION:
- What was it for?
- How was it used?
- Who used it?
- Where was it placed?
- Ritual or practical?

CONTENT:
- What does it represent?
- Symbols and meanings
- Cultural context
- Connection to nature
- Human experience

CONTEXT:
- When was it made?
- Who made it?
- What society?
- What environment?
- How does it connect to other works?
```

### Comparative Analysis

```
COMPARING PREHISTORIC WORKS:

SIMILARITIES:
- Human universal experiences
- Nature as subject
- Ritual purposes
- Community creation
- Skill and intention

DIFFERENCES:
- Regional materials
- Cultural meanings
- Specific techniques
- Local environment
- Chronological development

QUESTIONS TO ASK:
- Why might similar forms appear
  in different places?
- What does difference tell us?
- How do materials affect form?
- What can we know vs. speculate?
```

## ğŸ“ Key Takeaways

```
1. ART BEGINS WITH HUMANITY
   - Symbolic thinking
   - Deliberate creation
   - Beyond survival needs
   - Universal human trait

2. MATERIALS SHAPE POSSIBILITIES
   - Stone, bone, pigment
   - What's available locally
   - Techniques develop
   - Permanence varies

3. CONTEXT IS CRUCIAL
   - Environment matters
   - Social organization
   - Ritual practices
   - We often speculate

4. THEMES RECUR GLOBALLY
   - Animals as subjects
   - Human figures
   - Ritual/religious function
   - Status and hierarchy

5. INTERPRETATION IS LIMITED
   - No written records
   - Cultural distance
   - Multiple possibilities
   - Humility required
```

## ğŸ“ Exam Tips

```
FOR PREHISTORIC ART:

1. KNOW DATES AND LOCATIONS
   - Be specific about geography
   - Understand chronology
   - Place works in context

2. DESCRIBE CAREFULLY
   - What do you actually see?
   - Don't over-interpret
   - Use art vocabulary

3. DISCUSS FUNCTION
   - Ritual purposes
   - Community use
   - Status markers
   - Multiple possibilities

4. ACKNOWLEDGE UNCERTAINTY
   - "May have been used for..."
   - "Scholars believe..."
   - "Evidence suggests..."
   - Avoid definitive claims

5. COMPARE MEANINGFULLY
   - Why compare these works?
   - What does comparison reveal?
   - Similarities AND differences
```
