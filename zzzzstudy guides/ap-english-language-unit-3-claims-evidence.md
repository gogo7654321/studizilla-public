## üì¢ Types of Claims

### Claim Categories

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Fact/Existence** | Assert something is true | "Climate change is caused by humans" |
| **Definition** | Argue how to classify | "Esports should be considered a sport" |
| **Value** | Judge worth or quality | "Privacy is more important than security" |
| **Cause/Effect** | Explain relationships | "Social media causes anxiety in teens" |
| **Policy** | Recommend action | "Schools should ban phones" |
| **Comparison** | Evaluate similarities/differences | "Online learning is as effective as in-person" |

### Claim Hierarchy

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Main claim (thesis)** | Central argument | "The death penalty should be abolished" |
| **Supporting claims** | Reasons for thesis | "Innocent people have been executed" |
| **Sub-claims** | Specific points | "124 people were exonerated since 1973" |

### Characteristics of Strong Claims

| Characteristic | Weak Example | Strong Example |
|----------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Arguable** | "Pollution is bad" | "Carbon taxes will reduce emissions" |
| **Specific** | "Education needs reform" | "Schools should eliminate standardized testing" |
| **Defensible** | "Everything should be free" | "Community college should be tuition-free" |
| **Significant** | "Pizza tastes good" | "School lunch programs affect academic performance" |

### Qualifying Claims

| Qualifier | Function | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Often** | Frequency limit | "Social media often spreads misinformation" |
| **Many** | Quantity limit | "Many students struggle with anxiety" |
| **In some cases** | Situational limit | "In some cases, homework can be beneficial" |
| **Tends to** | Pattern limit | "Technology tends to increase productivity" |

## üìä Types of Evidence

### Evidence Categories

| Type | Definition | Example | Strength |
|------|------------|---------|----------|
| **Statistics** | Numerical data | "80% of teens use social media" | High |
| **Facts** | Verifiable information | "The law passed in 2020" | High |
| **Expert testimony** | Authority opinions | "Dr. Smith argues that..." | High |
| **Examples** | Specific instances | "In California, schools that..." | Medium |
| **Anecdotes** | Personal stories | "My grandmother experienced..." | Low alone |
| **Analogies** | Comparisons | "Like a house needs a foundation..." | Medium |
| **Hypotheticals** | Imagined scenarios | "Imagine if every student..." | Low alone |

### Evidence Quality

| Quality | Strong | Weak |
|---------|--------|------|
| **Relevance** | Directly supports claim | Tangentially related |
| **Credibility** | Reliable, expert source | Questionable origin |
| **Sufficiency** | Adequate amount | Insufficient support |
| **Accuracy** | Verified, current | Outdated, disputed |
| **Representativeness** | Typical example | Cherry-picked |

### Statistical Evidence

| Good Practice | Poor Practice |
|---------------|---------------|
| Include sample size | Vague numbers |
| Note source and date | Undated statistics |
| Provide context | Numbers without context |
| Explain methodology | No information on study |
| Acknowledge limitations | Presented as absolute |

### Expert Testimony

| Credible Expert | Less Credible |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Relevant credentials | Wrong field |
| Recent research | Outdated views |
| Peer-reviewed work | Self-published |
| Acknowledged by field | Fringe position |
| Discloses conflicts | Hidden interests |

## üîç Source Evaluation

### The CRAAP Test

| Criterion | Questions |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Currency** | When was it published? Has it been updated? Is it current for your topic? |
| **Relevance** | Does it relate to your topic? Who is the intended audience? Is it appropriate? |
| **Authority** | Who is the author? What are their credentials? Is the publisher reputable? |
| **Accuracy** | Is it supported by evidence? Has it been peer-reviewed? Can you verify claims? |
| **Purpose** | Why does this exist? Is it to inform, sell, persuade, entertain? Is there bias? |

### Source Types

| Source Type | Characteristics | Best For |
|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Scholarly journals** | Peer-reviewed, specialized | Academic claims |
| **Government reports** | Official data, comprehensive | Statistics, policy |
| **News organizations** | Current, accessible | Recent events |
| **Think tanks** | In-depth analysis | Policy arguments |
| **Books** | Comprehensive treatment | Background, context |
| **Websites** | Varies widely | Depends on site |

### Bias Detection

| Type of Bias | How to Identify | Example |
|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Political** | Check funding, affiliations | Think tank alignment |
| **Commercial** | Look for sponsors | Industry-funded studies |
| **Confirmation** | Cherry-picked evidence | Only citing agreeing sources |
| **Selection** | Unrepresentative samples | Excluding contrary data |
| **Language** | Loaded words | "Crisis" vs. "situation" |

### Primary vs. Secondary Sources

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Primary** | Original, firsthand | Letters, speeches, data sets, interviews |
| **Secondary** | Analysis of primary | Articles, textbooks, documentaries |
| **Tertiary** | Compiled from others | Encyclopedias, dictionaries |

## ‚úèÔ∏è Integrating Evidence

### Integration Methods

| Method | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Direct quote** | Exact words | Smith states, "Education is key" (45). |
| **Paraphrase** | Restated in your words | Smith argues that education matters (45). |
| **Summary** | Condensed main ideas | Smith's chapter discusses education reform (45-60). |

### When to Quote

| Quote | Paraphrase |
|-------|------------|
| Language is significant | Ideas matter more than words |
| Exact wording is contested | You can say it more clearly |
| Author's voice is important | Your voice should dominate |
| Definition or key term | General information |

### Signal Phrases

| Purpose | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| **Neutral** | states, notes, observes, reports, describes |
| **Agreement** | affirms, supports, confirms, validates |
| **Disagreement** | disputes, challenges, questions, refutes |
| **Argument** | argues, claims, contends, asserts, maintains |
| **Suggestion** | implies, suggests, hints, indicates |

### Embedding Quotes

| Poor Integration | Strong Integration |
|------------------|-------------------|
| "Education is key." | As Smith argues, "Education is key" to economic mobility (45). |
| Smith said, "Education is key." | Smith's research confirms that "education is key" to reducing inequality (45). |

### Quote Sandwich

| Layer | Function | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Introduction** | Context, signal phrase | According to education researcher Smith, |
| **Quote** | Evidence | "students from low-income families benefit most from early intervention" (45). |
| **Explanation** | Commentary | This finding suggests that targeted programs can break cycles of poverty. |

## üîó Synthesis

### Definition

| Synthesis | Combining multiple sources into a unified argument |
|-----------|--------------------------------------------------|

### Purpose

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Create new understanding** | Combine ideas in original ways |
| **Show relationships** | Connect sources to each other |
| **Support argument** | Use multiple voices for claim |
| **Add complexity** | Acknowledge nuance |

### Synthesis vs. Summary

| Summary | Synthesis |
|---------|-----------|
| Describes one source | Combines multiple sources |
| Reports what source says | Creates new meaning |
| Source-by-source | Idea-by-idea |
| "Source A says... Source B says..." | "Both sources suggest... However, they differ on..." |

### Synthesis Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Agreement** | Sources support same point | "Both Smith and Jones find that..." |
| **Disagreement** | Sources conflict | "While Smith argues X, Jones contends Y" |
| **Complementary** | Sources add to each other | "Smith's research on X is extended by Jones's work on Y" |
| **Qualification** | One limits another | "Although Smith finds X, Jones notes exceptions" |

### Synthesis Organization

| Pattern | Description | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Thematic** | Organize by ideas, not sources | Multiple sources on same topic |
| **Chronological** | Organize by time | Historical development |
| **Problem-solution** | Problem then solutions | Policy arguments |
| **Comparison** | Point-by-point analysis | Evaluating options |

## üìù The Synthesis Essay

### AP Synthesis Essay Structure

| Part | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Introduction** | Context + thesis that synthesizes sources |
| **Body 1** | Claim + evidence from 2+ sources + commentary |
| **Body 2** | Claim + evidence from 2+ sources + commentary |
| **Body 3** | Claim/counterargument + evidence + response |
| **Conclusion** | Synthesis of ideas + implications |

### Source Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Minimum sources** | Use at least 3 sources |
| **Integration** | Sources must support your argument |
| **Citation** | Reference sources by letter or author |
| **Balance** | Don't over-rely on one source |

### Sample Synthesis Moves

| Move | Template |
|------|----------|
| **Agreeing sources** | "Both Source A and Source C suggest that..." |
| **Conflicting sources** | "While Source B argues X, Source E challenges this by..." |
| **Adding nuance** | "Source D adds complexity by noting that..." |
| **Using counterargument** | "Although Source F claims..., this overlooks..." |

## üìù Sample Evidence Analysis

### Analyzing Statistical Evidence

**Example:** "According to the CDC, 42% of high school students report persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness."

| Analysis Element | Commentary |
|------------------|------------|
| **Source credibility** | CDC is authoritative government health agency |
| **Currency** | Check when data was collected |
| **Specificity** | Precise percentage, defined population |
| **Significance** | Nearly half of teens affected‚Äîmajor concern |
| **Limitations** | Self-reported; may underestimate due to stigma |

### Analyzing Expert Testimony

**Example:** "Dr. Jean Twenge, psychology professor at San Diego State University, argues that smartphone use directly correlates with teen depression."

| Analysis Element | Commentary |
|------------------|------------|
| **Authority** | University professor with relevant expertise |
| **Research basis** | Author of peer-reviewed studies |
| **Potential bias** | Check if author has agenda or conflicts |
| **How to use** | Cite credentials, connect to your claim |

### Analyzing Anecdotal Evidence

**Example:** "Maria, a 16-year-old from Ohio, deleted her social media accounts after experiencing severe anxiety, and within months reported feeling 'like myself again.'"

| Strength | Limitation |
|----------|------------|
| Humanizes the issue | One person's experience |
| Emotionally compelling | Not statistically significant |
| Specific, relatable | May not be representative |
| **Best use:** Combine with statistical evidence |

## üß© Advanced Evidence Strategies

### Layering Evidence

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Multiple types** | Combine statistics + anecdote | Data + human story |
| **Multiple sources** | Converging evidence | Three studies agree |

### Anticipating Objections to Evidence

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "That's just one study" | Cite corroborating research |
| "That source is biased" | Acknowledge perspective, show validity |
| "That's outdated" | Show relevance or cite current data |
| "That's anecdotal" | Combine with statistical evidence |

### Using Counterevidence

| Purpose | Strategy |
|---------|----------|
| **Concession** | Acknowledge opposing evidence, then refute |
| **Qualification** | Use to limit your claim appropriately |
| **Strengthening** | Show you've considered all evidence |
