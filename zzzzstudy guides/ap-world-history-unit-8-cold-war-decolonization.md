## ÔøΩüìã Unit Overview

The second half of the 20th century was dominated by the rivalry between the United States and Soviet Union - a "cold" war fought through ideology, proxy conflicts, and nuclear standoff. Simultaneously, the European colonial empires collapsed as independence movements swept Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. These two phenomena shaped the modern world! üåê

### Essential Questions

| Question | Focus |
|----------|-------|

| How did superpowers compete? | Proxy wars; ideology; space race |
| Why did empires collapse? | WWII weakened; nationalism rose |
| How did new nations emerge? | Varied paths to independence |
| What was the Non-Aligned Movement? | Third way between superpowers |
