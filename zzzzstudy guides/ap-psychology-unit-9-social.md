## âœï¸ Sample Free Response

**Question**: A student notices that classmates don't offer to help a struggling peer during group work. Explain how each of the following concepts from social psychology might account for this:

(a) Diffusion of responsibility
(b) Pluralistic ignorance
(c) Bystander effect
(d) Social loafing

**Answer**:

(a) **Diffusion of responsibility**: With multiple classmates present, each individual feels less personal responsibility to help. The responsibility is "diffused" among all group members, so each person thinks "someone else will help" or "it's not solely my job." If only one person witnessed the struggling peer, that individual would feel more obligated to assist.

(b) **Pluralistic ignorance**: Each classmate privately notices the struggling peer may need help but looks to others to determine if action is needed. When they see no one else helping, they incorrectly assume that the situation must not require intervention or that others don't think it's a problem. Everyone privately thinks help might be needed, but no one acts because no one else is acting.

(c) **Bystander effect**: The presence of multiple bystanders (classmates) reduces the likelihood that any individual will help. Research by LatanÃ© and Darley shows that as the number of bystanders increases, the probability of any one person helping decreases. If only one classmate were present, they would be more likely to help.

(d) **Social loafing**: In the group work context, individuals may reduce their effort because their individual contributions are less identifiable. The struggling peer's need for help becomes everyone's problem but no one's specific responsibility. Each person may assume others are handling it, leading to less overall effort to assist.
