## âš–ï¸ Excitatory vs. Inhibitory

(b) Identify the neurotransmitter most associated with movement and explain how damage affecting this neurotransmitter might impact Sarah.

(c) Explain how the sympathetic nervous system would have responded during the accident.

**Answer**:

(a) **Broca's area**: Located in the left frontal lobe, damage would cause difficulty producing speech (Broca's aphasia). Sarah could understand language but would speak slowly and with effort.

**Hippocampus**: Located in the limbic system, damage would impair Sarah's ability to form new explicit memories (anterograde amnesia). She might not remember events after the accident.

**Cerebellum**: Located at the back of the brain, damage would impair coordination and balance. Sarah might have difficulty walking smoothly or performing coordinated movements.

(b) **Dopamine** is the neurotransmitter most associated with movement. If Sarah's dopamine-producing neurons were damaged (especially in the substantia nigra), she might develop symptoms similar to Parkinson's disease, including tremors, slow movement, and difficulty initiating movement.

(c) During the accident, Sarah's **sympathetic nervous system** would have activated the "fight or flight" response:
- Heart rate would increase to pump more blood
- Breathing would accelerate
- Pupils would dilate to take in more light
- Adrenaline (epinephrine) would be released
- Blood would flow to muscles, away from digestion
- These responses prepared her body to respond to the emergency
