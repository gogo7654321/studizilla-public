## ‚ö° Energy Basics

### Forms of Energy

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Chemical** | Fossil fuels, food |
| **Kinetic** | Moving objects |
| **Potential** | Stored energy |
| **Thermal** | Heat |
| **Electrical** | Current flow |
| **Nuclear** | Atomic bonds |

### Energy Units

| Unit | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Joule (J)** | SI unit of energy |
| **Calorie** | Heat energy |
| **BTU** | British thermal unit |
| **Watt (W)** | Power (J/second) |
| **Kilowatt-hour** | Energy over time |

### First Law of Thermodynamics

| Law | Energy cannot be created or destroyed |
|-----|--------------------------------------|
| **Converted** | Changes form |
| **Conserved** | Total stays constant |

### Second Law of Thermodynamics

| Law | Energy conversions lose useful energy |
|-----|--------------------------------------|
| **Entropy** | Increases in all processes |
| **Heat** | Waste product |
| **Efficiency** | Always < 100% |

## ‚ò¢Ô∏è Nuclear Energy

### Nuclear Fission

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Definition** | Splitting heavy atoms |
| **Fuel** | Uranium-235 (or plutonium) |
| **Product** | Energy + smaller atoms |

### Nuclear Power Plant

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Reactor core** | Fission occurs |
| **Control rods** | Absorb neutrons, control reaction |
| **Coolant** | Transfers heat |
| **Steam generator** | Creates steam |
| **Turbine** | Steam spins turbine |
| **Generator** | Produces electricity |

### Advantages

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **No CO‚ÇÇ emissions** | During operation |
| **High energy density** | Small fuel amounts |
| **Reliable** | Base load power |

| Disadvantage | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Radioactive waste** | Long-lived, dangerous |
| **Accidents** | Chernobyl, Fukushima |
| **High capital cost** | Expensive to build |
| **Proliferation** | Weapons risk |
| **Limited fuel** | Uranium not renewable |

### Nuclear Waste

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Low-level** | Clothing, tools (stored on-site) |
| **High-level** | Spent fuel rods (long-term storage) |
| **Half-life** | Time for half to decay |

### Nuclear Accidents

| Event | Year | Cause |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Three Mile Island** | 1979 | Partial meltdown |
| **Chernobyl** | 1986 | Explosion, meltdown |
| **Fukushima** | 2011 | Earthquake, tsunami |

### Nuclear Fusion

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Definition** | Combining light atoms |
