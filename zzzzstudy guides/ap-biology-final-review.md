# üìò Unit 1: Chemistry of Life

## Essential Concepts

### Water Properties (COHESPA)
| Property | Cause | Biological Importance |
|----------|-------|----------------------|
| **C**ohesion | H-bonds between water | Water transport in plants |
| **O**smosis | Water diffusion | Cell homeostasis |

| **P**H buffering | H‚Å∫/OH‚Åª balance | Enzyme function |
| **A**dhesion | H-bonds to surfaces | Capillary action |

### pH Scale
- **pH = -log[H‚Å∫]**
- pH < 7 = acidic (more H‚Å∫)
- pH = 7 = neutral
- pH > 7 = basic (more OH‚Åª)
- Each pH unit = 10√ó difference in [H‚Å∫]

### Four Macromolecules Summary

| Macromolecule | Monomer | Bond Type | Elements | Functions |
|---------------|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Monosaccharides | Glycosidic | C, H, O | Energy, structure |
| **Lipids** | Fatty acids + glycerol | Ester | C, H, O | Membranes, energy storage, hormones |
| **Proteins** | Amino acids | Peptide | C, H, O, N, S | Enzymes, structure, transport |
| **Nucleic Acids** | Nucleotides | Phosphodiester | C, H, O, N, P | Genetic info, energy (ATP) |

### Protein Structure Levels
1. **Primary:** Amino acid sequence
2. **Secondary:** Œ±-helix, Œ≤-pleated sheet (H-bonds)
3. **Tertiary:** 3D folding (R-group interactions)
4. **Quaternary:** Multiple polypeptide chains

### Enzyme Kinetics
- **Induced fit model:** Enzyme changes shape upon substrate binding
- **Factors affecting rate:** Temperature, pH, substrate concentration, enzyme concentration
- **Competitive inhibitor:** Binds active site
- **Noncompetitive inhibitor:** Binds allosteric site, changes shape
- **Feedback inhibition:** End product inhibits pathway enzyme

# üìò Unit 3: Cellular Energetics

## Energy & Metabolism Overview

### Laws of Thermodynamics
1. **First Law:** Energy cannot be created or destroyed
2. **Second Law:** Entropy always increases; energy transfers are inefficient

### Free Energy Equation
$$ŒîG = ŒîH - TŒîS$$
- **ŒîG < 0:** Exergonic (spontaneous, releases energy)
- **ŒîG > 0:** Endergonic (requires energy input)
- **ŒîG = 0:** At equilibrium

### ATP Structure & Function
- Adenine + Ribose + 3 Phosphates
- Energy released when terminal phosphate bond broken
- ATP ‚Üî ADP + P·µ¢ (ŒîG = -7.3 kcal/mol)

## Cellular Respiration

### Overview Equation
$$C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 ‚Üí 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{~36-38 ATP}$$

### Stage Comparison Table

| Stage | Location | Input | Output |

# üìò Unit 4: Cell Communication & Cell Cycle

## Cell Signaling Overview

### Three Stages of Cell Signaling
1. **Reception:** Signal molecule binds receptor
2. **Transduction:** Signal relay via cascade
3. **Response:** Cellular activity changes

### Signal Types by Distance

| Type | Distance | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Autocrine** | Same cell | Immune cell self-stimulation |
| **Paracrine** | Nearby cells | Growth factors |
| **Endocrine** | Long distance (blood) | Hormones |
| **Synaptic** | Across synapse | Neurotransmitters |
| **Direct contact** | Cell-to-cell | Gap junctions, plasmodesmata |

### Receptor Types

| Receptor | Location | Mechanism |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **G-protein coupled** | Membrane | G-protein activates enzymes (cAMP cascade) |
| **Receptor tyrosine kinase** | Membrane | Dimerization, phosphorylation cascade |
| **Ligand-gated ion channel** | Membrane | Ion flow changes |
| **Intracellular receptor** | Cytoplasm/Nucleus | Direct gene regulation (steroids) |

### Second Messengers
- **cAMP:** Activates protein kinase A
- **Ca¬≤‚Å∫:** Released from ER, activates various proteins
- **IP‚ÇÉ:** Triggers Ca¬≤‚Å∫ release
- **DAG:** Activates protein kinase C

## Cell Cycle

### Phases Overview

| Phase | Events | Duration |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **G‚ÇÅ** | Cell growth, organelle duplication | Variable |
| **S** | DNA replication | ~8 hours |
| **G‚ÇÇ** | Preparation for division | ~4 hours |
| **M (Mitosis)** | Nuclear division | ~1 hour |
| **Cytokinesis** | Cytoplasm division | Overlaps with M |

### Mitosis Stages (PMAT)

| Stage | Events | Key Feature |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Prophase** | Chromatin condenses, nuclear envelope breaks down | Spindle forms |
| **Metaphase** | Chromosomes align at metaphase plate | Maximum condensation |
| **Anaphase** | Sister chromatids separate, move to poles | Centromeres divide |
| **Telophase** | Nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense | Cleavage furrow begins |

### Cell Cycle Regulation

**Checkpoints:**
- **G‚ÇÅ checkpoint:** Is cell big enough? DNA damaged?
- **G‚ÇÇ checkpoint:** Is DNA replicated correctly?
- **M checkpoint (spindle):** Are chromosomes attached?

**Key Regulators:**
- **Cyclins:** Proteins that fluctuate during cycle
- **CDKs (Cyclin-dependent kinases):** Enzymes activated by cyclins
- **p53:** Tumor suppressor, arrests cycle if DNA damaged
- **Rb:** Tumor suppressor, blocks S phase entry

### Cancer Connection
- **Proto-oncogenes ‚Üí Oncogenes:** Accelerate cell cycle (gain of function)
- **Tumor suppressors:** Brake on cell cycle (loss of function)
- **Characteristics:** Uncontrolled division, metastasis, angiogenesis

## Apoptosis (Programmed Cell Death)
- Controlled cell death for development/health
- Caspase cascade activation
- Cell shrinks, fragments, engulfed by phagocytes
- Triggered by: DNA damage, no survival signals, death signals

# üìò Unit 5: Heredity

## Meiosis Overview

### Comparison: Mitosis vs. Meiosis

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Divisions** | 1 | 2 |
| **Daughter cells** | 2 | 4 |
| **Chromosome number** | Diploid (2n) | Haploid (n) |
| **Genetic variation** | Clones | Varied |
| **Purpose** | Growth, repair | Gamete production |
| **Crossing over** | No | Yes (Prophase I) |

### Meiosis I vs. Meiosis II

| | Meiosis I | Meiosis II |
|-|-----------|------------|
| **Starts with** | Diploid (2n) | Haploid (n) |
| **Ends with** | Haploid (n) | Haploid (n) |
| **Separation** | Homologous pairs | Sister chromatids |
| **Crossing over** | Yes (Prophase I) | No |

### Sources of Genetic Variation
1. **Crossing over:** Prophase I, exchange of segments
2. **Independent assortment:** Random homolog orientation
3. **Random fertilization:** Any sperm + any egg
4. **Mutations:** Changes in DNA sequence

## Mendelian Genetics

### Key Terms
- **Allele:** Version of a gene
- **Dominant:** Expressed when heterozygous (capital letter)
- **Recessive:** Only expressed when homozygous (lowercase)
- **Homozygous:** Same alleles (AA or aa)
- **Heterozygous:** Different alleles (Aa)
- **Phenotype:** Physical trait
- **Genotype:** Genetic makeup

### Mendel's Laws
1. **Law of Segregation:** Allele pairs separate during meiosis
2. **Law of Independent Assortment:** Genes on different chromosomes assort independently

### Cross Types & Ratios

| Cross | Genotypic Ratio | Phenotypic Ratio |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| Monohybrid (Aa √ó Aa) | 1:2:1 | 3:1 |
| Dihybrid (AaBb √ó AaBb) | 1:2:1:2:4:2:1:2:1 | 9:3:3:1 |
| Testcross (Aa √ó aa) | 1:1 | 1:1 |

### Chi-Square Analysis
$$œá¬≤ = Œ£ \frac{(O - E)¬≤}{E}$$
- Compare calculated œá¬≤ to critical value
- If œá¬≤ < critical value ‚Üí Accept null hypothesis (fits expected)
- Degrees of freedom = categories - 1

## Non-Mendelian Genetics

| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Incomplete dominance** | Blended phenotype | Pink flowers (RW) |
| **Codominance** | Both alleles fully expressed | AB blood type |
| **Multiple alleles** | More than 2 alleles in population | ABO blood |
| **Polygenic** | Multiple genes affect one trait | Skin color, height |
| **Epistasis** | One gene affects another's expression | Coat color in mice |
| **Pleiotropy** | One gene affects multiple traits | Sickle cell anemia |
| **Sex-linked** | Genes on X or Y chromosome | Color blindness, hemophilia |

### Sex-Linked Inheritance
- Most on X chromosome (X-linked)
- Males (XY) express all X-linked alleles
- Females (XX) can be carriers
- Pattern: Affected males, carrier mothers, rare affected females

### Linked Genes & Recombination
- Genes on same chromosome tend to be inherited together
- Recombination frequency = (recombinants / total) √ó 100
- 1% recombination = 1 map unit (centiMorgan)

# üìò Unit 6: Gene Expression & Regulation

## DNA Structure & Replication

### DNA vs. RNA Comparison

| Feature | DNA | RNA |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Sugar | Deoxyribose | Ribose |
| Bases | A, T, G, C | A, U, G, C |
| Structure | Double helix | Usually single-stranded |
| Location | Nucleus | Nucleus and cytoplasm |
| Function | Genetic information | Protein synthesis |

### DNA Replication
- **Semi-conservative:** Each new DNA has one old, one new strand
- **Origin of replication:** Where replication begins
- **Helicase:** Unwinds DNA
- **Primase:** Makes RNA primers
- **DNA polymerase III:** Adds nucleotides 5'‚Üí3'
- **Leading strand:** Continuous synthesis
- **Lagging strand:** Okazaki fragments (discontinuous)
- **DNA ligase:** Joins Okazaki fragments

### Replication Enzymes Table

| Enzyme | Function |
|--------|----------|
| Helicase | Unwinds double helix |
| Topoisomerase | Relieves tension ahead of fork |
| Primase | Synthesizes RNA primer |
| DNA Pol III | Main synthesis enzyme |
| DNA Pol I | Replaces primers with DNA |
| Ligase | Joins fragments |
| SSB proteins | Stabilize single strands |

## Transcription (DNA ‚Üí RNA)

### Stages
1. **Initiation:** RNA polymerase binds promoter (TATA box)
2. **Elongation:** RNA synthesized 5'‚Üí3' (template read 3'‚Üí5')
3. **Termination:** Reaches terminator sequence

### Prokaryotic vs. Eukaryotic Transcription

| Feature | Prokaryotes | Eukaryotes |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Location | Cytoplasm | Nucleus |
| RNA polymerase | One type | Three types |
| mRNA processing | None | Yes (cap, tail, splicing) |
| Coupled with translation | Yes | No |

### mRNA Processing (Eukaryotes)
1. **5' cap:** Modified G added (ribosome binding, stability)
2. **3' poly-A tail:** ~200 A's added (stability, export)
3. **Splicing:** Introns removed, exons joined (spliceosome)

### Alternative Splicing
- Different exon combinations = different proteins
- One gene ‚Üí multiple proteins
- Increases protein diversity

## Translation (RNA ‚Üí Protein)

### Components
- **mRNA:** Carries genetic code
- **tRNA:** Carries amino acids, has anticodon
- **rRNA:** Ribosome structure and catalysis
- **Ribosomes:** A site (aminoacyl), P site (peptidyl), E site (exit)

### Genetic Code Features
- **Triplet:** 3 nucleotides = 1 codon = 1 amino acid
- **Degenerate:** Multiple codons for same amino acid
- **Universal:** Same code in almost all organisms
- **Start codon:** AUG (methionine)
- **Stop codons:** UAA, UAG, UGA

### Translation Stages
1. **Initiation:** Small subunit + mRNA + initiator tRNA (AUG)
2. **Elongation:** tRNA delivers amino acids, peptide bonds form
3. **Termination:** Stop codon, release factor, ribosome dissociates

## Gene Regulation

### Prokaryotic Regulation: Operons

|------|-----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Lac operon** (inducible) | Repressor binds operator | OFF | Lactose induces (removes repressor) |

### Eukaryotic Gene Regulation Levels

|-------|-----------|

| **Transcriptional** | Transcription factors, enhancers, silencers |
| **Post-transcriptional** | Alternative splicing, mRNA degradation, miRNA |
| **Translational** | Initiation factors, mRNA stability |
| **Post-translational** | Protein modification, degradation |

### Epigenetics
- **DNA methylation:** CH‚ÇÉ groups silence genes
- **Histone acetylation:** Opens chromatin (gene activation)
- **Histone methylation:** Can activate or silence
- Modifications inherited but can be changed by environment

## Mutations

### Types of Point Mutations

| Type | Effect | Severity |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Silent** | Same amino acid | None |
| **Missense** | Different amino acid | Variable |
| **Nonsense** | Stop codon | Usually severe |

### Causes of Mutations
- **Spontaneous:** Replication errors
- **Induced:** Mutagens (UV, chemicals, radiation)

| **Analogous** | Different origin, same function | Bat wing, insect wing |
| **Vestigial** | Reduced, little/no function | Human appendix, whale pelvis |

## Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

### Equations
$$p + q = 1$$
$$p¬≤ + 2pq + q¬≤ = 1$$

- p = frequency of dominant allele
- q = frequency of recessive allele
- p¬≤ = frequency of homozygous dominant
- 2pq = frequency of heterozygotes
- q¬≤ = frequency of homozygous recessive

### Five Conditions for Equilibrium
1. No mutations
2. Random mating
3. No natural selection
4. Large population
5. No migration (gene flow)

**If ANY condition violated ‚Üí Evolution occurs**

## Mechanisms of Evolution

| Mechanism | Description | Effect on Variation |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Natural selection** | Differential survival/reproduction | Reduces (directional) or maintains (balancing) |
| **Genetic drift** | Random changes in allele frequency | Reduces (especially in small populations) |
| **Gene flow** | Migration between populations | Increases within, decreases between |
| **Mutation** | New alleles | Increases |
| **Sexual selection** | Mate choice | Can increase or decrease |

### Types of Natural Selection

| Type | Effect | Graph Shape |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Directional** | Favors one extreme | Curve shifts |
| **Stabilizing** | Favors average | Curve narrows |
| **Disruptive** | Favors both extremes | Curve splits |

### Genetic Drift Types
- **Bottleneck effect:** Population crash reduces diversity
- **Founder effect:** Small group colonizes, limited diversity

## Speciation

## Phylogenetics

### Reading Phylogenetic Trees
- **Clade:** Group with common ancestor + all descendants
- **Node:** Common ancestor point
- **Outgroup:** Most distantly related species
- **Sister taxa:** Closest relatives
- **Monophyletic:** Valid clade
- **Paraphyletic:** Excludes some descendants
- **Polyphyletic:** No common ancestor

### Molecular Clocks
- Mutations accumulate at relatively constant rate
- More differences = more evolutionary time
- Used to estimate divergence times

## Origin of Life

### Timeline
1. Abiotic synthesis of small organic molecules
2. Formation of polymers
3. Development of self-replicating molecules (RNA world)
4. Packaging into protocells

### Endosymbiotic Theory
- Mitochondria and chloroplasts were once free-living prokaryotes

# üìò Unit 8: Ecology

## Ecological Hierarchy
Individual ‚Üí Population ‚Üí Community ‚Üí Ecosystem ‚Üí Biome ‚Üí Biosphere

## Population Ecology

### Population Growth Models

| Model | Equation | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Exponential** | dN/dt = rN | Unlimited growth, J-curve |
| **Logistic** | dN/dt = rN(K-N)/K | Limited by carrying capacity, S-curve |

### Key Terms
- **r:** Per capita growth rate
- **K:** Carrying capacity
- **N:** Population size

### r vs. K Selection

| r-selected | K-selected |
|------------|------------|
| Many offspring | Few offspring |
| Little parental care | High parental care |
| Short lifespan | Long lifespan |
| Early maturity | Late maturity |
| Small size | Large size |
| Unstable environments | Stable environments |
| Example: Insects, bacteria | Example: Elephants, whales |

### Survivorship Curves
- **Type I:** Low early mortality (humans)
- **Type II:** Constant mortality (birds)
- **Type III:** High early mortality (fish)

### Population Regulation
- **Density-dependent:** Competition, predation, disease
- **Density-independent:** Weather, natural disasters

## Community Ecology

### Species Interactions

| Interaction | Species 1 | Species 2 | Example |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Mutualism** | + | + | Bee and flower |
| **Commensalism** | + | 0 | Barnacles on whale |
| **Parasitism** | + | - | Tick and dog |
| **Predation** | + | - | Lion and zebra |
| **Competition** | - | - | Two species, same niche |

### Ecological Niche
- **Fundamental niche:** Full range of conditions species CAN occupy
- **Realized niche:** Actual conditions (limited by competition)
- **Competitive exclusion:** No two species with identical niche coexist

### Keystone Species
- Disproportionate effect on community
- Removal causes major ecosystem changes
- Examples: Sea otters, wolves, beavers

### Ecological Succession

| Type | Starting Point | Examples |
|------|---------------|----------|
| **Primary** | No soil (bare rock) | Volcanic island, glacier retreat |
| **Secondary** | Soil present | After fire, abandoned farm |

Stages: Pioneer species ‚Üí Intermediate ‚Üí Climax community

## Ecosystem Ecology

### Energy Flow
- **Producers (autotrophs):** Convert light to chemical energy
- **Consumers (heterotrophs):** Eat other organisms
- **Decomposers:** Break down dead organic matter
- **Only ~10% energy transferred** between trophic levels

### Trophic Levels
1. Producers
2. Primary consumers (herbivores)
3. Secondary consumers (carnivores)
4. Tertiary consumers (top carnivores)
5. Decomposers (all levels)

### Ecological Pyramids
- **Energy:** Always upright (10% rule)
- **Biomass:** Usually upright
- **Numbers:** Can be inverted (one tree, many insects)

### Biogeochemical Cycles
