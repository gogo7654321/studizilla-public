## üåø Eutrophication

### Definition

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Eutrophication** | Excess nutrients ‚Üí algal growth ‚Üí oxygen depletion |

### Process

| Step | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| **1** | Excess N and P enter water |
| **2** | Algae bloom (rapid growth) |
| **3** | Algae block light, plants die |
| **4** | Algae die and decompose |
| **5** | Decomposers use up oxygen |
| **6** | Fish and organisms die (dead zone) |

### Dead Zones

| Location | Cause |
|----------|-------|
| **Gulf of Mexico** | Mississippi River nutrients |
| **Chesapeake Bay** | Agricultural runoff |
| **Baltic Sea** | Fertilizer pollution |

### Hypoxia

| Definition | Dissolved oxygen < 2 mg/L |
|------------|---------------------------|
| **Cause** | Eutrophication |
| **Effect** | Most organisms can't survive |

### Cultural Eutrophication

| Definition | Human-caused eutrophication |
|------------|----------------------------|
| **Sources** | Fertilizers, sewage, detergents |

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Biological** | Bacteria decompose organics |

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Advanced** | Additional filtration |
| **Chemical** | Removes nutrients (N, P) |

|-----------|----------|
| **Septic tank** | Solids settle, decompose |

## üîã Bioaccumulation and Biomagnification

### Bioaccumulation

| Definition | Pollutants build up in organism |
|------------|--------------------------------|
| **Process** | Intake faster than excretion |
| **Examples** | Mercury, DDT, PCBs |

### Biomagnification

| Definition | Concentration increases up food chain |
|------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Each level** | Concentration multiplies |
| **Top predators** | Highest concentrations |

### Example: DDT

| Trophic Level | DDT Concentration |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Water** | 0.000003 ppm |
| **Phytoplankton** | 0.04 ppm |
| **Small fish** | 0.5 ppm |
| **Large fish** | 2 ppm |
| **Fish-eating birds** | 25 ppm |

### Effects

| Organism | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Birds** | Eggshell thinning (DDT) |
| **Fish** | Mercury poisoning |
| **Humans** | Neurological, developmental |

**(a)** Describe the process of eutrophication and explain why fish kills occur. (4 points)

**(c)** Explain why the fish kills happen in summer specifically. (2 points)

**(d)** Propose two agricultural practices and two policy solutions to reduce eutrophication. (4 points)

| Part | Key Points |
|------|------------|
