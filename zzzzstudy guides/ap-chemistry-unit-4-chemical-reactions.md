## ğŸš« 4.3 Limiting Reagent

### What is a Limiting Reagent? ğŸ”’

The reactant that runs out first and stops the reaction:

```
    LIMITING REAGENT ANALOGY
    
    Making sandwiches: 2 bread + 1 cheese â†’ 1 sandwich
    
    Have: 10 bread, 4 cheese
    
    Bread could make: 10/2 = 5 sandwiches
    Cheese could make: 4/1 = 4 sandwiches
    
    Cheese is LIMITING (runs out first)
    Bread is in EXCESS (some left over)
    
    Actually make: 4 sandwiches
```

### Finding the Limiting Reagent ğŸ”

**Method 1**: Compare moles to required ratio

**Method 2**: Calculate product from each reactant - smallest amount is actual yield

### Limiting Reagent Example ğŸ“

**4.0 mol Hâ‚‚ reacts with 2.5 mol Oâ‚‚. Find limiting reagent.**

$$\text{2H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{2H}_2\text{O}$$

|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Hâ‚‚ | 4.0 | $4.0 \times \frac{2}{2} = 4.0$ mol |
| Oâ‚‚ | 2.5 | $2.5 \times \frac{2}{1} = 5.0$ mol |

**Hâ‚‚ is limiting** (produces less product)

### Excess Reagent Calculation ğŸ“Š

- Hâ‚‚ used: 4.0 mol (all of it)
- Oâ‚‚ used: $4.0 \text{ mol H}_2 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol O}_2}{2 \text{ mol H}_2} = 2.0$ mol

## ğŸ“ˆ 4.4 Percent Yield

### Types of Yield ğŸ“Š

| Yield Type | Definition | How Obtained |
|------------|------------|--------------|
| **Theoretical yield** | Maximum possible amount | Calculated from stoichiometry |
| **Actual yield** | Amount actually produced | Measured experimentally |
| **Percent yield** | Efficiency of reaction | Calculated |

### Percent Yield Formula

$$\text{Percent yield} = \frac{\text{Actual yield}}{\text{Theoretical yield}} \times 100\%$$

### Why Less Than 100%? ğŸ¤”

- Side reactions
- Incomplete reactions
- Loss during transfer
- Impure reactants
- Equilibrium not favoring products

### Example Calculation ğŸ“

**A reaction should produce 25.0 g product (theoretical). Only 21.3 g is obtained (actual). What is the percent yield?**

$$\text{Percent yield} = \frac{21.3}{25.0} \times 100\% = 85.2\%$$
