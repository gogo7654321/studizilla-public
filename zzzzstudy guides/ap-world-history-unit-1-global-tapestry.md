## ‚úçÔ∏è Sample Multiple Choice

### Question 1

The spread of Islam to Southeast Asia was primarily accomplished through:

(A) Military conquest by Arab armies
(B) Merchant activity and Sufi missionaries
(C) Forced conversion by local rulers
(D) European colonization

**Answer: (B)** Islam spread to Southeast Asia through Indian Ocean trade networks, carried by merchants and Sufi missionaries who established communities in port cities.

### Question 2

Which of the following best describes a similarity between the Aztec and Inca empires?

(A) Both used writing systems to keep records
(B) Both relied on tribute or labor from conquered peoples
(C) Both had decentralized political structures
(D) Both were primarily trading empires

**Answer: (B)** Both empires extracted resources from conquered peoples - Aztecs through tribute, Incas through mit'a labor.

### Question 3

Neo-Confucianism in Song China differed from earlier Confucianism primarily in its:

(A) Rejection of civil service examinations
(B) Incorporation of Buddhist and Daoist concepts
(C) Focus on military rather than scholarly values
(D) Opposition to hierarchical social structures

**Answer: (B)** Neo-Confucianism blended traditional Confucian ideas with Buddhist and Daoist philosophical concepts.

## ‚úçÔ∏è Sample Short Answer Question (SAQ)

**Question**: Answer parts a, b, and c.

a) Identify ONE way that the Indian Ocean trade network facilitated cultural exchange between 1200 and 1450.

b) Identify ONE way that the Silk Road facilitated cultural exchange between 1200 and 1450.

c) Explain ONE significant difference between how ideas spread along the Indian Ocean trade routes versus the Silk Road.

**Sample Answer**:

a) The Indian Ocean trade network facilitated cultural exchange through the spread of Islam from Arabia to East Africa and Southeast Asia. Muslim merchants established trading communities in port cities like Kilwa and Malacca, where they intermarried with locals and spread their religious practices, creating syncretic cultures like the Swahili civilization.

b) The Silk Road facilitated cultural exchange through the transmission of technologies like paper, gunpowder, and the compass from China to the Islamic world and eventually Europe. These innovations traveled along with trade goods, carried by merchants who stopped at caravanserais where they exchanged knowledge.

c) A significant difference was the speed and volume of exchange. Indian Ocean maritime trade was faster and could carry more goods, leading to larger diasporic communities and more rapid cultural integration. The Silk Road's overland routes were slower and more dangerous, making cultural exchange more gradual and often carried by intermediaries rather than direct contact between distant cultures.
