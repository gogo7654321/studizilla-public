## üéØ Sub-Theme 1: Pre-Colonial African Civilizations

### Major African Kingdoms and Empires

```
WEST AFRICAN EMPIRES:

GHANA EMPIRE (300-1200 CE):
- Located between Sahara and forests
- Wealth from gold and salt trade
- "Land of Gold"
- Strong centralized government
- Declined due to drought and invasion

MALI EMPIRE (1235-1600 CE):
- Founded by Sundiata Keita
- Mansa Musa (r. 1312-1337)
- Timbuktu as center of learning
- Islam spread through trade
- Vast territorial expansion

SONGHAI EMPIRE (1464-1591 CE):
- Largest West African empire
- Askia Muhammad's reforms
- University of Sankore
- Conquered by Moroccan forces
- Advanced bureaucracy
```

## üåä Sub-Theme 2: The Transatlantic Slave Trade

### Origins and Development

```
TIMELINE:

1440s:
Portuguese begin trading on African coast

1492:
Columbus arrives in Americas
Beginning of European colonization

1502:
First enslaved Africans arrive in Americas

1619:
First Africans arrive in English Virginia

1700s:
Height of the slave trade
~6 million Africans transported

1807-1808:
Britain and US ban slave trade
(Trade continues illegally)

1865:
Slavery abolished in US
(13th Amendment)
```

### The Middle Passage

```
CONDITIONS:
- Voyages lasted 6-8 weeks
- Extreme overcrowding
- Minimal food and water
- Disease rampant
- Mortality rate: 15-20%
- Suicide attempts common
- Resistance and rebellions

ROUTES:
Africa ‚Üí Caribbean ‚Üí Americas
(Triangular Trade)

NUMBERS:
- 12.5 million Africans embarked
- ~10.7 million survived crossing
- 1.8 million died in transit
- Brazil received largest number
- ~400,000 to North America
```

### African Regions Affected

| Region | Percentage | Primary Destinations |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| West Central Africa | 45% | Brazil, Caribbean |
| Bight of Benin | 16% | Caribbean, Brazil |
| Bight of Biafra | 13% | Virginia, Jamaica |
| Gold Coast | 10% | Jamaica, Barbados |
| Senegambia | 6% | South Carolina |
| Sierra Leone | 4% | South Carolina |

## ‚õìÔ∏è Sub-Theme 3: Enslavement and Resistance

### Forms of Resistance in Africa

```
BEFORE CAPTURE:
- Armed resistance to raiders
- Diplomatic negotiations
- Strategic alliances
- Fortified villages
- Early warning systems

DURING TRANSPORT:
- Rebellions on slave ships
- Suicide as resistance
- Refusal to eat
- Attacks on crew
- The Amistad revolt (1839)

AT EMBARKATION:
- Port rebellions
- Escape attempts
- Community protection
- Religious resistance
```

### African Agency and Complexity

```
IMPORTANT CONTEXT:

AFRICAN PARTICIPATION:
- Some African leaders sold captives
- Wars produced prisoners of war
- Debt and criminal punishment
- Complex political calculations
- NOT racial slavery initially

RESISTANCE BY AFRICANS:
- Many kingdoms refused participation
- Some protected refugees
- Queen Nzinga's resistance
- Dahomey's complex role
- Internal African debates

UNDERSTANDING COMPLEXITY:
- Africans were agents, not just victims
- Pre-existing slavery was different
- Transatlantic trade transformed practices
- Power dynamics were complex
- European demand drove expansion
```

- Adaptation and transformation
