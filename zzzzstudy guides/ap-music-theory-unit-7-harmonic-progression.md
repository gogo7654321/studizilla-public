## üéµ 7.1 Secondary Dominants

### Concept of Tonicization

```
TONICIZATION:
Temporarily treating a chord as tonic
Without actually changing key

SECONDARY DOMINANT:
V of a chord other than I
"Borrows" dominant function

EXAMPLE (C major):
```

### Common Secondary Dominants


### Writing Secondary Dominants

```

1. Identify target chord (X)
2. Build major triad on its 5th
3. Add 7th if desired (V7/X)

EXAMPLE: V7/vi in C major
Target: A minor (vi)
Fifth of A = E
Build: E-G‚ôØ-B-D
Result: E7 = V7/vi
```

### Secondary Dominant Resolution

```
RESOLUTION PRINCIPLES:

1. RAISED NOTES GO UP
   F‚ôØ in V/V ‚Üí G (leading tone)
   
2. 7TH GOES DOWN
   D in V7/V ‚Üí C (7th resolves)

3. ROOT MOVES BY 5TH
   D ‚Üí G (authentic cadence)

V/V ‚Üí V ‚Üí I (common sequence)
"Five of five to five to one"
```

## üåê 7.3 Modulation

### Types of Modulation

```
MODULATION:
Actual key change (not just tonicization)

TYPES:

1. PIVOT CHORD MODULATION
   Common chord in both keys
   Smooth transition

2. DIRECT MODULATION
   Abrupt key change
   No pivot chord

3. SEQUENTIAL MODULATION
   Pattern leads to new key
   
4. CHROMATIC MODULATION
   Chromatic voice leading
   Enharmonic reinterpretation
```

### Pivot Chord Modulation

```
PIVOT CHORD:
Chord functioning in both keys

C MAJOR ‚Üí G MAJOR:

C major:  I   vi   ii    V   I
          C   Am   Dm    G   C
                    ‚Üì
G major:       [ii = v]
               Dm is ii in C
               Dm is v in G

Analysis:
C: I - vi - ii  |
G:      [v] - I - V - I
         pivot
```

### Common Modulation Targets

```
CLOSELY RELATED KEYS:
Differ by one accidental

FROM MAJOR KEY:
- Dominant (up P5): C ‚Üí G
- Subdominant (up P4): C ‚Üí F
- Relative minor (down m3): C ‚Üí Am
- ii minor: C ‚Üí Dm
- iii minor: C ‚Üí Em

FROM MINOR KEY:
- Relative major (up m3): Am ‚Üí C
- Dominant (with raised 7): Am ‚Üí Em
- Subdominant: Am ‚Üí Dm
- III major: Am ‚Üí C
- VII major: Am ‚Üí G
```

### Recognizing Modulation

```
SIGNS OF MODULATION:

1. NEW ACCIDENTALS
   Consistent sharps or flats
   Not just chromatic passing
   
2. CADENCE IN NEW KEY
   PAC confirms new tonic
   
3. NEW LEADING TONE
   7th of new key appears
   
4. PIVOT POINT
   Chord that could be in either key

LENGTH MATTERS:
Brief = Tonicization
Extended = Modulation
```
