# AP Comparative Government and Politics Final Exam Review

## Introduction to Comparative Politics

Comparative politics examines how different countries structure their governments, make political decisions, and respond to challenges. The AP Comparative Government course focuses on **six core countries**: China, Iran, Mexico, Nigeria, Russia, and the United Kingdom. Each represents different regime types, political cultures, and developmental paths.

### Why Compare?
- Identify patterns across political systems
- Understand how institutions shape political outcomes
- Analyze the relationship between culture and governance
- Evaluate different approaches to common challenges
- Develop analytical frameworks for understanding any political system

# Unit 1: Political Systems, Regimes, and Governments

## Core Concepts

### Regime Types

**Democracy**: Government where power derives from the people through free and fair elections
- **Liberal Democracy**: Protects individual rights, rule of law, separation of powers
- **Illiberal Democracy**: Elections occur but civil liberties are restricted

**Authoritarianism**: Power concentrated in a leader or small group, limited political freedoms
- Single-party states
- Military regimes
- Personalist dictatorships
- Theocracies

**Totalitarianism**: Extreme authoritarianism with complete control over public and private life

### Legitimacy

**Sources of Legitimacy**:
| Source | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Traditional | Based on customs, heredity, divine right | UK monarchy |
| Charismatic | Based on leader's personal qualities | Mao Zedong, Ayatollah Khomeini |
| Rational-Legal | Based on codified laws and procedures | Constitution-based systems |
| Performance | Based on government effectiveness | China's economic growth |
| Ideological | Based on belief in governing ideology | Iran's theocracy |

### State vs. Nation vs. Government

| Concept | Definition | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| State | Political entity with sovereignty over territory | United Kingdom |
| Nation | Group sharing cultural/ethnic identity | Kurds, Yoruba |
| Government | Institutions exercising state authority | Parliament, Congress |
| Nation-State | State boundaries align with national identity | Japan (relatively) |
| Multi-national State | Multiple nations within one state | Nigeria, Russia |

### Sovereignty

**Internal Sovereignty**: Supreme authority within borders
**External Sovereignty**: Recognition by other states
**Challenges to Sovereignty**:
- Globalization
- Supranational organizations (EU)
- Ethnic separatism
- Failed states

# Unit 2: Political Institutions

## Executive Structures

### Head of State vs. Head of Government

| Country | Head of State | Head of Government | Relationship |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **UK** | Monarch (King/Queen) | Prime Minister | Separate (ceremonial vs. executive) |
| **Mexico** | President | President | Same person |
| **Nigeria** | President | President | Same person |
| **Russia** | President | Prime Minister | President dominant |
| **Iran** | Supreme Leader | President | Supreme Leader dominant |
| **China** | President (General Secretary) | Premier | Party leader dominant |

### Executive Systems Explained

**Parliamentary System (UK)**:
- Executive emerges from legislature
- Prime Minister leads cabinet
- Vote of no confidence can remove government
- Fusion of powers
- Monarch serves ceremonial role

**Presidential System (Mexico, Nigeria)**:
- Separate election of executive
- Fixed terms
- President cannot be removed by legislature (except impeachment)
- Separation of powers
- President serves as both head of state and government

**Semi-Presidential System (Russia)**:
- Elected president with significant powers
- Prime Minister appointed by president
- President can dismiss parliament
- Dual executive structure

**Theocratic/Hybrid System (Iran)**:
- Supreme Leader holds ultimate authority (unelected, lifetime)
- Elected president has limited powers
- Guardian Council vets candidates
- Religious authority supersedes elected officials

**Single-Party System (China)**:
- Communist Party dominates all institutions
- General Secretary of CCP most powerful position
- State Council handles government administration
- Premier manages economic policy

# Unit 4: Party and Electoral Systems

## Electoral Systems

### Types of Electoral Systems

**Plurality/Majority Systems**:
- **First-Past-the-Post (FPTP)**: UK, Nigeria
  - Winner takes all in single-member districts
  - Tends toward two-party dominance
  - Geographic representation
  - "Wasted" votes for losers

- **Two-Round System**: France (not in AP6, but concept important)
  - If no majority, top two compete in runoff

**Proportional Representation (PR)**:
- Seats allocated based on vote percentage
- Multi-member districts
- Encourages multiparty systems
- Used in Russia (Duma, mixed)

**Mixed Systems**:
- **Mexico**: 300 SMD + 200 PR
- **Russia**: 225 SMD + 225 PR
- Combines geographic and proportional elements

### Electoral Systems by Country

| Country | Legislative System | Executive Election |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **UK** | FPTP (Commons) | Indirectly elected (parliamentary) |
| **Mexico** | Mixed (300 SMD + 200 PR) | Plurality (one round) |
| **Nigeria** | FPTP | Majority + geographic distribution |
| **Russia** | Mixed (225 SMD + 225 PR) | Two-round majority |
| **Iran** | Two-round majority | Two-round majority |
| **China** | No competitive elections | N/A |

# FRQ Response Strategies

## Types of FRQs

### Type 1: Conceptual Analysis
*Apply a concept to countries*

**Example**: "Define legitimacy and explain how two of the AP6 countries derive their legitimacy differently."

**Strategy**:
1. Define the concept clearly
2. Identify examples from two countries
3. Explain the connection explicitly
4. Compare/contrast if applicable

### Type 2: Country Comparison
*Compare specific features across countries*

**Example**: "Compare the electoral systems of Mexico and the United Kingdom."

**Strategy**:
1. Describe System A clearly
2. Describe System B clearly
3. Identify similarities
4. Identify differences
5. Explain implications of differences

### Type 3: Cause and Effect
*Explain political phenomena*

**Example**: "Explain two reasons why Nigeria has lower voter turnout than the United Kingdom."

**Strategy**:
1. State reason 1 clearly
2. Provide evidence/examples
3. State reason 2 clearly
4. Provide evidence/examples
5. Connect to broader patterns

### Type 4: Argument Development
*Take and defend a position*

**Example**: "Some argue that economic development leads to democratization. Use evidence from two AP6 countries to evaluate this claim."

**Strategy**:
1. State your position
2. Evidence supporting the claim
3. Evidence complicating the claim
4. Nuanced conclusion
