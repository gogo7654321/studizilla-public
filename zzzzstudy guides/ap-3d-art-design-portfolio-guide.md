## üéØ Portfolio Components

### Three Sections

```
AP 3-D PORTFOLIO STRUCTURE:

1. SUSTAINED INVESTIGATION
   - 15 digital images
   - Written statement (1,200 characters)
   - Inquiry-based exploration
   - Shows growth and development
   - 60% of score

2. SELECTED WORKS
   - 5 works
   - 2 images each (10 total images)
   - Shows mastery of 3-D design
   - 40% of score

3. PHYSICAL PORTFOLIO (Optional)
   - May be requested for validation
   - Keep original works accessible

TOTAL: 25 images submitted digitally
```

## üìã Sustained Investigation

### Choosing Your Inquiry

```
STRONG 3-D INQUIRY QUESTIONS:

FORM-BASED:
"How does negative space define
 positive form?"

MATERIAL-BASED:
"What happens when organic materials
 are used in architectural contexts?"

CONCEPTUAL:
"How can physical weight communicate
 emotional burden?"

SPATIAL:
"How does scale alter the relationship
 between viewer and object?"

PROCESS-BASED:
"What forms emerge from additive vs.
 subtractive processes?"
```

### 3-D Investigation Phases

```
DEVELOPMENT STRUCTURE:

PHASE 1: EXPLORATION (Images 1-4)
- Material experiments
- Scale studies
- Form explorations
- Process testing

PHASE 2: FOCUS (Images 5-8)
- Narrowing inquiry
- Technique refinement
- Conceptual development
- Connection building

PHASE 3: DEVELOPMENT (Images 9-12)
- Complex problem-solving
- Scale advancement
- Risk-taking
- Synthesis emerging

PHASE 4: RESOLUTION (Images 13-15)
- Sophisticated solutions
- Mastery demonstration
- Culminating works
- Complete expression
```

### Documenting 3-D Process

```
WHAT TO DOCUMENT:

MAQUETTES/MODELS:
- Scale studies
- Material tests
- Structural experiments
- Form variations

WORK IN PROGRESS:
- Construction stages
- Material preparation
- Assembly process
- Problem-solving moments

MULTIPLE VIEWS:
- Front, back, sides
- Top (if relevant)
- Detail shots
- Relationship to environment

SCALE REFERENCE:
- Human figure (when appropriate)
- Common objects
- Hands working
- Environmental context

```

```
- Best reveals form

SECONDARY VIEW:

- Adds information
- Shows different aspect

- Choose most informative
- Reveal dimensionality

```

### Lighting 3-D Work

```
LIGHTING TECHNIQUES:

DIRECTIONAL LIGHT:
- Creates shadows for form
- Reveals texture
- Models surface
- Dramatic effect

DIFFUSED LIGHT:
- Softer shadows
- Even illumination
- Reduces contrast
- Accurate color

BACKLIGHTING:
- Silhouette
- Transparency
- Dramatic effect
- Special cases

AVOID:
- Flash on camera
- Harsh overhead
- Multiple confusing shadows
- Flat, even wash
```
