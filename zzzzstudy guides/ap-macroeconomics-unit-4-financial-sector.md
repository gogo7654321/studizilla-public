## ðŸ§® Money Multiplier Derivations

### Simple Money Multiplier

**Key Insight:** Each dollar of reserves supports multiple dollars of deposits

```
Reserve Requirement = 10% (or 0.10)

Round 1: $1,000 deposit
â”œâ”€â”€ Required reserves: $100 (kept)
â””â”€â”€ Excess reserves: $900 (loaned out)

Round 2: $900 becomes deposit elsewhere
â”œâ”€â”€ Required reserves: $90
â””â”€â”€ Excess reserves: $810 (loaned out)

Round 3: $810 becomes deposit
â”œâ”€â”€ Required reserves: $81
â””â”€â”€ Excess reserves: $729 (loaned out)

... continues ...
```

**Mathematical Derivation:**
$$\text{Total Deposits} = \$900 \times (1 + 0.9 + 0.9^2 + 0.9^3 + ...)$$

$$= \$900 \times \frac{1}{1-0.9} = \$900 \times 10 = \$9,000$$

$$\boxed{\text{Money Multiplier} = \frac{1}{RR} = \frac{1}{0.10} = 10}$$

### Important Distinctions

| Scenario | Maximum Î”Money Supply |
|----------|----------------------|
| **New deposit** | (Deposit - Required Reserves) Ã— Multiplier |
| **Fed buys bonds** | Purchase Amount Ã— Multiplier |
| **Fed changes RR** | Affects multiplier size itself |

### Example Calculations

```

Maximum Î”MS = $50 million Ã— 5 = $250 million
```
