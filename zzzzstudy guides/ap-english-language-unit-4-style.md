## üìù Diction (Word Choice)

### Levels of Diction

| Level | Characteristics | Example |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| **Formal** | Academic, sophisticated | "The institution necessitates systematic reform" |
| **Informal** | Casual, conversational | "The school really needs to change" |
| **Colloquial** | Everyday speech | "School's gotta shake things up" |
| **Slang** | Group-specific, trendy | "That class slaps" |
| **Jargon** | Technical, specialized | "The pedagogy requires scaffolding" |
| **Archaic** | Old-fashioned | "Verily, education doth require..." |

### Diction Analysis Categories

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Denotation** | Dictionary meaning | House = dwelling structure |
| **Connotation** | Associated meaning | Home = warmth, belonging |
| **Abstract** | Concepts, ideas | Freedom, justice, love |
| **Concrete** | Tangible, specific | Red brick house, oak tree |
| **General** | Broad category | Vehicle, animal, food |
| **Specific** | Precise example | 1967 Mustang, golden retriever |

### Connotation Spectrum

| Positive | Neutral | Negative |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Thrifty | Economical | Cheap |
| Assertive | Confident | Aggressive |
| Curious | Interested | Nosy |
| Youthful | Young | Immature |
| Slim | Thin | Scrawny |
| Aroma | Smell | Stench |

## üìê Syntax (Sentence Structure)

### Sentence Types

| Type | Definition | Example | Effect |
|------|------------|---------|--------|
| **Simple** | One independent clause | "She ran." | Direct, clear |
| **Compound** | Two+ independent clauses | "She ran, and he followed." | Balanced, connected |
| **Complex** | Independent + dependent | "When she ran, he followed." | Shows relationships |
| **Compound-complex** | Both types combined | "When she ran, he followed, and they disappeared." | Sophisticated, nuanced |

### Sentence Length

| Length | Effect | Use |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Short** | Emphasis, urgency, drama | Climax, key points |
| **Medium** | Readable, balanced | Most prose |
| **Long** | Complex ideas, building | Description, analysis |
| **Varied** | Rhythm, interest | Skilled writing |

### Sentence Beginnings

| Beginning | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Subject-verb** | "She walked slowly" | Direct, conventional |
| **Adverb** | "Slowly, she walked" | Emphasis on manner |
| **Prepositional phrase** | "In the darkness, she walked" | Sets scene |
| **Participial phrase** | "Walking slowly, she..." | Action emphasis |
| **Subordinate clause** | "Although tired, she walked" | Shows relationship |
| **Transitional word** | "However, she walked" | Logical connection |

### Punctuation Effects

| Punctuation | Effect | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| **Period** | Full stop, finality | Statements, emphasis |
| **Comma** | Pause, connection | Lists, complexity |
| **Semicolon** | Balance, connection | Related ideas |
| **Colon** | Anticipation, introduction | Lists, explanations |
| **Dash** | Interruption, emphasis | Asides, drama |
| **Ellipsis** | Trailing off, omission | Hesitation, mystery |
| **Exclamation** | Intensity, emotion | Excitement, urgency |
| **Question** | Engagement, inquiry | Rhetorical effect |

### Syntax Patterns

| Pattern | Definition | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Parallelism** | Repeated structure | "I came, I saw, I conquered" |
| **Anaphora** | Repeated beginnings | "We shall fight... We shall fight..." |
| **Epistrophe** | Repeated endings | "...of the people, by the people, for the people" |
| **Antithesis** | Contrasting structure | "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times" |
| **Chiasmus** | Reversed parallelism | "Ask not what your country can do for you" |
| **Asyndeton** | Omitting conjunctions | "I came, I saw, I conquered" |
| **Polysyndeton** | Excess conjunctions | "And the thunder and the lightning and the rain" |

## üåü Figurative Language

### Comparison Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Simile** | Comparison using like/as | "Her smile was like sunshine" |
| **Metaphor** | Direct comparison | "Life is a journey" |
| **Extended metaphor** | Sustained comparison | Throughout a paragraph or work |
| **Personification** | Human traits to non-human | "The wind whispered secrets" |
| **Anthropomorphism** | Animals as humans | Talking animals in fables |
| **Analogy** | Extended comparison for explanation | "The brain is like a computer" |

### Sound Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Alliteration** | Repeated consonant sounds (beginning) | "Peter Piper picked peppers" |
| **Assonance** | Repeated vowel sounds | "The rain in Spain" |
| **Consonance** | Repeated consonant sounds (middle/end) | "All's well that ends well" |
| **Onomatopoeia** | Words imitating sounds | "Buzz, hiss, splash" |
| **Euphony** | Pleasant sounds | "Soft, silken, silver" |
| **Cacophony** | Harsh sounds | "Crackle, grating, jarring" |

### Emphasis Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Hyperbole** | Exaggeration | "I've told you a million times" |
| **Understatement** | Deliberate minimizing | "The Titanic had a small issue" |
| **Litotes** | Negative understatement | "Not bad" (meaning good) |
| **Rhetorical question** | Question not expecting answer | "Who doesn't love freedom?" |
| **Apostrophe** | Address to absent/abstract | "O Death, where is thy sting?" |

### Indirect Meaning Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Irony** | Meaning opposite of words | Saying "Great weather" in a storm |
| **Sarcasm** | Biting, contemptuous irony | "Oh, brilliant idea" |
| **Satire** | Mocking to critique | Swift's "A Modest Proposal" |
| **Paradox** | Apparent contradiction | "The only thing I know is that I know nothing" |
| **Oxymoron** | Contradictory terms together | "Deafening silence" |

### Reference Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Allusion** | Reference to known work/event | "He's a Romeo" |
| **Allegory** | Extended symbolic narrative | Animal Farm |
| **Symbolism** | Object representing idea | Dove = peace |
| **Metonymy** | Associated name | "The White House said..." |
| **Synecdoche** | Part for whole | "All hands on deck" |

## üìù Comparative Style Analysis

### Hemingway vs. Faulkner

| Element | Hemingway | Faulkner |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Diction** | Simple, concrete, monosyllabic | Complex, abstract, Latinate |

## üìä Style Imitation Exercises

### Exercise 1: Sentence Combining

**Original sentences:**
- The sun set.
- The sky turned orange.
- The birds flew home.

**Hemingway style:** The sun set. The sky was orange. The birds flew home.

**Faulkner style:** As the sun, that dying orb of the day's last heat and light, descended below the horizon, the sky, once blue and now transformed, took on the burning orange hue of a final fire, while the birds, those dark-winged creatures of the air, turned in their tireless flight toward home.

### Exercise 2: Diction Substitution

**Original:** The politician talked about the problem.

| Register | Revised |
|----------|---------|
| **Formal** | The senator addressed the crisis |
| **Informal** | The guy mentioned the issue |
| **Academic** | The elected representative analyzed the systemic challenge |
| **Journalistic** | The lawmaker discussed the controversy |

### Exercise 3: Syntax Variation

**Original:** I went to the store. I bought milk. I came home.

| Style | Revised |
|-------|---------|
| **Combined** | I went to the store, bought milk, and came home. |
| **Subordinated** | After going to the store to buy milk, I came home. |
| **Inverted** | To the store I went, and with milk I returned home. |
| **Fragmented** | The store. Milk. Home again. |
