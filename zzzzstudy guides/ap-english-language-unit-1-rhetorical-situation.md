## üìñ Essential Rhetorical Devices

### Repetition Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Anaphora** | Repetition at beginning | "We shall fight...We shall fight...We shall fight" |
| **Epistrophe** | Repetition at end | "...of the people, by the people, for the people" |
| **Anadiplosis** | End word begins next clause | "Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate." |
| **Symploce** | Anaphora + epistrophe combined | "When there is talk of hatred, let us stand up. When there is talk of violence, let us stand up." |

### Sound Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Alliteration** | Repeated consonant sounds | "Peter Piper picked a peck" |
| **Assonance** | Repeated vowel sounds | "The rain in Spain" |
| **Consonance** | Repeated consonant sounds (middle/end) | "All's well that ends well" |

### Syntax Devices

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Parallelism** | Repeated grammatical structure | "I came, I saw, I conquered" |
| **Antithesis** | Contrasting ideas in balanced structure | "Ask not what your country can do for you‚Äîask what you can do for your country" |
| **Chiasmus** | Reversed parallelism | "Never let a fool kiss you or a kiss fool you" |
| **Asyndeton** | Omitting conjunctions | "I came, I saw, I conquered" |
| **Polysyndeton** | Excessive conjunctions | "And I will and I can and I must" |
