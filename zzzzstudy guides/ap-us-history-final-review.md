### Period 1: 1491-1607 (5% of Exam)

#### Key Concepts
- **Native American Diversity**: Hundreds of distinct cultures, languages, and economies
- **Columbian Exchange**: Transfer of plants, animals, diseases between hemispheres
- **Spanish Colonial System**: Encomienda, missions, casta system
- **European Motivations**: "God, Gold, and Glory"

#### Essential Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Columbian Exchange | Biological and cultural exchange between Old and New Worlds |
| Encomienda | Spanish labor system granting colonists Native labor |
| Mestizo | Person of mixed European and Native ancestry |
| Casta System | Spanish colonial racial hierarchy |
| Pueblo Peoples | Sedentary agricultural societies in Southwest |

#### Theme Connections (Period 1)
- **MIG**: European exploration and settlement patterns
- **GEO**: Geographic diversity shaped Native societies
- **WXT**: Economic motives driving colonization
- **SOC**: Racial hierarchies established early

### Period 2: 1607-1754 (10% of Exam)

#### Key Concepts
- **British Colonial Regions**: New England, Middle, Southern colonies
- **Transatlantic Trade**: Mercantilism, triangular trade, slavery
- **Colonial Society**: Religious diversity, social hierarchy, emerging identity
- **Native Relations**: Varying from cooperation to conflict

#### Colonial Comparison Chart

| Aspect | New England | Middle | Southern |
|--------|-------------|--------|----------|
| Economy | Shipbuilding, fishing, trade | Grain, trade, manufacturing | Tobacco, rice, indigo |
| Religion | Puritan theocracy | Religious diversity | Anglican, less strict |
| Labor | Family farms, some slavery | Mixed labor systems | Slavery, indentured servants |
| Society | Town-centered, education focus | Diverse, commercial | Plantation hierarchy |

#### Key Documents
- **Mayflower Compact (1620)**: Self-government precedent
- **Fundamental Orders of Connecticut (1639)**: First written constitution
- **Maryland Toleration Act (1649)**: Early religious tolerance

#### Important Figures
- John Winthrop - "City on a Hill"
- Anne Hutchinson - Challenged Puritan authority
- William Penn - Quaker colony of tolerance
- Metacom (King Philip) - Led Native resistance

#### Theme Connections (Period 2)
- **NAT**: Colonial identities forming
- **POL**: Self-government traditions developing
- **SOC**: Slavery becoming entrenched
- **CUL**: Great Awakening unifying colonies

### Period 3: 1754-1800 (12% of Exam)

#### Key Concepts
- **French and Indian War**: Shifted British-colonial relations
- **Revolutionary Ideology**: Enlightenment influence, republicanism
- **Constitution**: Federalism, separation of powers, compromises
- **Early Republic**: Party formation, foreign policy challenges

#### Road to Revolution
1. **French and Indian War** ‚Üí British debt, changed attitudes
2. **Sugar/Stamp Acts** ‚Üí "No taxation without representation"
3. **Townshend Acts** ‚Üí Colonial boycotts, committees of correspondence
4. **Intolerable Acts** ‚Üí First Continental Congress
5. **Lexington & Concord** ‚Üí Armed conflict begins

#### Constitutional Compromises
| Issue | Compromise |
|-------|------------|
| Representation | Great Compromise: bicameral legislature |
| Slavery counting | 3/5 Compromise |
| Slave trade | 20-year protection |
| Executive | Electoral College |

#### Key Documents
- **Declaration of Independence (1776)**: Natural rights philosophy
- **Articles of Confederation (1781)**: Weak central government
- **Constitution (1787)**: Federal system with checks and balances
- **Bill of Rights (1791)**: Protected individual liberties
- **Federalist Papers**: Defended Constitution

#### Important Figures
- George Washington - Set presidential precedents
- Thomas Jefferson - Declaration author, Democratic-Republicans
- Alexander Hamilton - Financial system, Federalists
- James Madison - "Father of the Constitution"
- John Adams - XYZ Affair, Alien & Sedition Acts

#### Theme Connections (Period 3)
- **POL**: New government structures created
- **NAT**: American identity crystallizing
- **WOR**: Neutrality in European conflicts
- **SOC**: Revolutionary ideals vs. slavery contradiction

## ‚úçÔ∏è DBQ STRATEGIES

### Understanding the DBQ
- **Time**: 60 minutes (includes 15-minute reading period)
- **Documents**: Usually 7 documents
- **Scoring**: Thesis, Contextualization, Evidence, Analysis, Complexity

### DBQ Writing Template

#### Introduction (5-7 minutes writing)
```
1. Contextualization (1-2 sentences of background)
2. Thesis statement (must take a position and include categories)
```

**Sample Thesis Formula**:
> Although [counterargument], [your position] because [reason 1], [reason 2], and [reason 3].

#### Body Paragraphs (35-40 minutes)
```
For each paragraph:
1. Topic sentence (connects to thesis)
2. Document evidence (cite 2-3 documents)
3. Analysis (explain how documents support your point)
4. Outside evidence (1+ piece per essay minimum)
5. HAPP analysis for sourcing (at least 3 documents)
```

**HAPP (Sourcing)**:
- **H**istorical Situation: What's happening when this was created?
- **A**udience: Who was this meant for?
- **P**urpose: Why was this created?
- **P**oint of View: What's the creator's perspective?

#### Conclusion (3-5 minutes)
```
1. Restate thesis (different words)
2. Address complexity (connections, contradictions, multiple perspectives)
```

### Earning Complexity Point
- Explain nuance by analyzing multiple perspectives
- Connect to different time period or geographic area
- Qualify or modify an argument
- Explain relevant contradictions or tensions

## ‚úçÔ∏è LEQ STRATEGIES

### Understanding the LEQ
- **Time**: 40 minutes
- **Choice**: 3 questions, choose 1
- **Scoring**: Thesis, Contextualization, Evidence, Analysis, Complexity

### LEQ Writing Template

#### Introduction
```
1. Contextualization (2-3 sentences minimum)
2. Clear thesis that responds to the prompt
```

#### Body Paragraphs (3 recommended)
```
For each paragraph:
1. Topic sentence
2. Specific historical evidence (names, dates, events)
3. Analysis connecting evidence to thesis
4. Explanation of significance
```

#### Conclusion
```
1. Synthesize argument
2. Connect to broader themes or other periods
```

### Common LEQ Types

| Type | Key Phrase | Strategy |
|------|------------|----------|
| Causation | "caused," "led to," "resulted in" | Show cause and effect chain |
| Comparison | "similarities and differences" | Use comparative structure |
| Continuity & Change | "extent to which," "changed over time" | Show what stayed same and changed |
| Periodization | "turning point" | Argue what changed and why it matters |
