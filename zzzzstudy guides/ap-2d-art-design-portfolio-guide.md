# üé® AP 2-D Art and Design Portfolio Guide

## üìö Course Overview

AP 2-D Art and Design is a portfolio-based course focusing on two-dimensional design concepts including graphic design, photography, printmaking, collage, and digital art.

---

## üéØ Portfolio Components

### Three Sections

```
AP 2-D PORTFOLIO STRUCTURE:

1. SUSTAINED INVESTIGATION
   - 15 digital images
   - Written statement (1,200 characters)
   - Inquiry-based exploration
   - Shows growth and development
   - 60% of score

2. SELECTED WORKS
   - 5 works
   - 2 images each (10 total images)
   - Shows mastery of design
   - 40% of score

3. PHYSICAL PORTFOLIO (Optional for some)
   - May be requested for validation
   - Keep originals accessible

TOTAL: 25 images submitted digitally
```

### Scoring Criteria

```
SCORING RUBRIC (6-point scale):

6 - EXCEPTIONAL
    Sophisticated, inventive, complex
    Outstanding visual evidence
    Masterful decision-making

5 - STRONG
    Effective and sustained inquiry
    Consistent evidence of skill
    Thoughtful experimentation

4 - GOOD
    Consistent investigation
    Adequate skill demonstration
    Shows development

3 - MODERATE
    Some investigation present
    Uneven development
    Basic skill level

2 - WEAK
    Minimal investigation
    Limited skill evidence
    Incomplete development

1-0 - POOR/NO RESPONSE
    Little to no evidence
```

## üìã Sustained Investigation

### What It Is

```
SUSTAINED INVESTIGATION:

PURPOSE:
- Show inquiry-guided art making
- Demonstrate growth over time
- Explore ideas in depth
- Document decision-making

REQUIREMENTS:
- 15 images showing process
- Clear progression
- Written statement
- Coherent theme or question

THIS IS NOT:
- Random collection of work
- Unrelated pieces
- Only final products
- Surface-level exploration
```

### Choosing Your Inquiry

```
STRONG INQUIRY QUESTIONS:

PERSONAL:
"How does my cultural identity 
 shape my visual expression?"

CONCEPTUAL:
"How can repetition create 
 psychological tension?"

MATERIAL:
"What happens when traditional 
 techniques meet digital tools?"

SOCIAL:
"How do consumer symbols 
 construct identity?"

FORMAL:
"How does negative space 
 activate composition?"
```

### Developing Your Investigation

```
INVESTIGATION PHASES:

PHASE 1: EXPLORATION (Images 1-4)
- Initial ideas and experiments
- Material/media testing
- Brainstorming visually
- Finding your direction

PHASE 2: FOCUS (Images 5-8)
- Narrowing your inquiry
- Deeper exploration
- Refining techniques
- Building connections

PHASE 3: DEVELOPMENT (Images 9-12)
- Complex problem-solving
- Risk-taking
- Synthesis of ideas
- Challenging yourself

PHASE 4: RESOLUTION (Images 13-15)
- Sophisticated solutions
- Mastery demonstration
- Culminating works
- Reflection on journey
```

### Process Documentation

```
DOCUMENTING PROCESS:

WHAT TO INCLUDE:
- Sketches and studies
- Material experiments
- Detail shots
- Works in progress
- Comparative stages
- Failed experiments that informed work

HOW TO PHOTOGRAPH:
- Clean, neutral background
- Even lighting
- No glare or shadows
- Accurate color
- High resolution
- Proper orientation
```

### Requirements

```
SELECTED WORKS SECTION:

5 WORKS TOTAL:

- View 1: Overall composition
- View 2: Detail or alternate view

- Demonstrate mastery
- Show range within 2-D design
- Highlight best work
- Different from sustained investigation
  (can share some works)

SELECTION CRITERIA:
- Technical excellence
- Strong design principles
- Effective communication
- Personal voice
```

### Photographing 2-D Work

```
PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS:

SETUP:
- Camera parallel to work
- No perspective distortion
- Crop to edges of work
- Include full composition

LIGHTING:
- Even, diffused light
- No hot spots
- True color representation
- Minimize texture shadows
  (unless intentional)

DETAILS:
- Show texture/mark-making
- Highlight craftsmanship
- Capture material qualities
- Demonstrate technique

DIGITAL WORK:
- Screenshot at high resolution
- Show without software interface
- Include different views if applicable
```

## ‚úçÔ∏è Written Statement

### Statement Requirements

```
WRITTEN STATEMENT:

LENGTH: 1,200 characters maximum
(Including spaces)

CONTENT MUST ADDRESS:
1. What is your inquiry?
2. How did you explore it?
3. What did you discover?
4. How did work evolve?

FORMAT:
- Clear, concise language
- First person ("I")
- Active voice preferred
- Specific examples
- No art jargon for its own sake
```

### Writing Strategies

```
STATEMENT STRUCTURE:

OPENING (200-300 characters):
Introduce your inquiry
What question drove your work?

MIDDLE (600-700 characters):
Describe your process
How did you explore?
What experiments/risks?
How did work develop?

CLOSING (200-300 characters):
What did you learn?
How did understanding evolve?
Where might this lead?

AVOID:
- Vague generalizations
- Listing media only
- Describing obvious visuals
- Not connecting to inquiry
```

### Sample Statement Elements

```
STRONG STATEMENT ELEMENTS:

INQUIRY INTRO:
"My investigation explores how memory
distorts familiar spaces..."

PROCESS DESCRIPTION:
"I began by photographing childhood
locations, then manipulated scale and
color to visualize how recollection
alters perception..."

DEVELOPMENT:
"As I worked, I discovered that
removing architectural details
created more emotional impact than
adding them..."

CONCLUSION:
"This investigation revealed that
absence often communicates presence
more powerfully..."
```

## üîÑ Revision and Growth

### Showing Development

```
EVIDENCE OF GROWTH:

TECHNICAL GROWTH:
- Skill improvement visible
- Media mastery increasing
- Craftsmanship developing

CONCEPTUAL GROWTH:
- Ideas becoming complex
- Connections deepening
- Understanding evolving

RISK-TAKING:
- Trying new approaches
- Learning from "failures"
- Pushing boundaries

SYNTHESIS:
- Combining discoveries
- Building on earlier work
- Creating connections
```

### Revision Strategies

```
REVISING YOUR PORTFOLIO:

1. SEQUENCE REVIEW
   Does order show development?
   Is progression clear?
   Does it tell a story?

2. QUALITY CHECK
   Are images high quality?
   Is documentation clear?
   Does work reproduce well?

3. VARIETY ASSESSMENT
   Is there enough range?
   Are experiments visible?
   Is there depth AND breadth?

4. STATEMENT ALIGNMENT
   Does writing match images?
   Are claims supported?
   Is language precise?

5. PEER FEEDBACK
   Get fresh eyes
   Ask specific questions
   Consider outside perspective
```

## üì∏ Image Submission

### Technical Requirements

```
IMAGE SPECIFICATIONS:

RESOLUTION:
- High quality (at least 1200px 
  on longest side)
- Proper file format (JPEG)
- No compression artifacts

COMPOSITION:
- Work fills frame appropriately
- Proper cropping
- Correct orientation
- No distortion

COLOR:
- Accurate color representation
- Proper white balance
- Not over/under exposed

FILE NAMING:
- Follow AP guidelines exactly
- Include required information
- Organized and consistent
```

### Common Submission Errors

```
AVOID THESE MISTAKES:

PHOTOGRAPHY ERRORS:
- Blurry images
- Keystoning/distortion
- Uneven lighting
- Visible glare
- Wrong orientation

CONTENT ERRORS:
- Not 15 images for SI
- Not showing process
- Disconnected works
- No clear inquiry

DOCUMENTATION ERRORS:
- Wrong file format
- Poor resolution
- Incorrect naming
- Missing information

STATEMENT ERRORS:
- Over character limit
- Not addressing prompts
- Vague language
- Grammar/spelling errors
```
