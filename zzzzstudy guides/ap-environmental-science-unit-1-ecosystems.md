## ‚ö° Energy Flow

### The 10% Rule

| Principle | |
|-----------|--|
| **Only 10%** | Energy transfers to next trophic level |
| **90% lost** | Heat, metabolism, movement |
| **Result** | Fewer organisms at higher levels |

### Trophic Levels

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Producer (1st)** | Make own food | Grass, algae |
| **Primary consumer (2nd)** | Eat producers | Rabbit, zooplankton |
| **Secondary consumer (3rd)** | Eat primary consumers | Snake, small fish |
| **Tertiary consumer (4th)** | Eat secondary consumers | Hawk, tuna |
| **Quaternary (5th)** | Top predators | Orca, human |

### Energy Pyramid

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Wide base** | Most energy at producer level |
| **Narrow top** | Least energy at top predators |
| **Never inverted** | Always more energy at bottom |

### Types of Pyramids

| Pyramid | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Energy** | Amount of energy (never inverted) |
| **Biomass** | Mass of organisms (usually not inverted) |
| **Numbers** | Count of individuals (can be inverted) |

## üåä Productivity

### Types of Productivity

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Gross Primary Productivity (GPP)** | Total energy captured by producers |
| **Net Primary Productivity (NPP)** | GPP - Respiration |
| **Net Primary Productivity** | Energy available to consumers |

### Formula

| NPP | = GPP - R |
|-----|-----------|
| **GPP** | Total photosynthesis |
| **R** | Producer respiration |

### High NPP Ecosystems

| Ecosystem | NPP |
|-----------|-----|
| **Tropical rainforest** | Very high |
| **Estuaries** | Very high |
| **Swamps/marshes** | High |
| **Coral reefs** | High |

### Low NPP Ecosystems

| Ecosystem | NPP |
|-----------|-----|
| **Open ocean** | Low (but huge area) |
| **Desert** | Very low |
| **Tundra** | Low |

### Limiting Factors

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Sunlight** | Energy for photosynthesis |
| **Water** | Essential for life |
| **Nutrients** | N, P often limiting |
| **Temperature** | Affects metabolism |

## üî¥ Phosphorus Cycle

### Unique Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **No atmospheric phase** | Only sedimentary cycle |

| **Often limiting** | In aquatic systems |

| Reservoir | Form |
|-----------|------|
| **Rocks** | Phosphate minerals |

| **Organisms** | DNA, ATP, bones |

### Phosphorus Processes

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| **Decomposition** | Returns P to soil |

### Human Impacts

| Activity | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Fertilizers** | Excess phosphorus |
| **Detergents** | Contain phosphates |

| **Result** | Eutrophication |

| Definition | |
|------------|--|

| Stage | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Pioneer** | First colonizers |
| **Intermediate** | Growing diversity |
| **Climax community** | Stable, mature ecosystem |

## üìù Free Response Question Practice

### FRQ Practice 1: Energy Flow

**Scenario:** A grassland ecosystem receives 1,000,000 kcal/m¬≤/year of solar energy.

**(a)** Calculate the energy available to tertiary consumers if producers capture 1% of solar energy. Show your work. (3 points)

**(b)** Explain why a food chain rarely has more than 4-5 trophic levels. (2 points)

**(c)** Predict what would happen to secondary consumer populations if a disease eliminated half the primary consumers. Justify your answer. (2 points)

**(d)** Describe one human activity that disrupts energy flow in ecosystems and propose a solution. (2 points)

### Sample Answer Rubric:

| Part | Key Points |
|------|------------|
| **(a)** | 1,000,000 √ó 0.01 = 10,000 (producers) ‚Üí 1,000 ‚Üí 100 ‚Üí **10 kcal/m¬≤/yr** |
| **(b)** | Energy decreases at each level (10% rule); not enough energy to support another level |
| **(c)** | Secondary consumers would decrease; less prey available; competition increases |
| **(d)** | Deforestation removes producers; solution: reforestation, sustainable forestry |

### FRQ Practice 2: Biogeochemical Cycles

**Scenario:** A farmer applies nitrogen fertilizer to crops each spring.

**(a)** Trace the path of a nitrogen atom from fertilizer to the atmosphere. Include at least 4 processes. (4 points)

**(b)** Explain how excess nitrogen fertilizer affects aquatic ecosystems downstream. (3 points)

**(c)** Compare the nitrogen cycle to the phosphorus cycle. Identify two key differences. (2 points)

**(d)** Propose two sustainable farming practices that would reduce nitrogen pollution. (2 points)

### FRQ Practice 3: Ecological Succession

**Scenario:** A volcanic eruption covers an island with lava.

**(a)** Identify this type of succession and explain why. (2 points)

**(b)** Describe the role of pioneer species in this ecosystem. Provide two examples. (3 points)

**(c)** Predict the sequence of plant communities that would develop over 500 years. (3 points)

**(d)** Compare how this succession would differ if a forest fire had burned the island instead. (2 points)
