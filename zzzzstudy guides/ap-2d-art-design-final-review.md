## üìù Written Statement Guide

### Statement Framework

```
1,200 CHARACTERS TEMPLATE:

OPENING (~200-250 characters):
"My investigation explores [topic/question]
through [media/approach]. I was drawn to
this inquiry because [personal connection]."

PROCESS (~400-500 characters):
"I began by [initial experiments]. As I
worked, I discovered [finding]. This led
me to explore [development]. I took risks
by [experimentation], which revealed
[insight]."

SYNTHESIS (~300-400 characters):
"Through this investigation, I learned
[understanding]. My work evolved from
[beginning] to [end], showing how [growth].
This process deepened my understanding
of [concept]."
```

### Strong Statement Examples

```
EFFECTIVE PHRASES:

INQUIRY LANGUAGE:
- "I questioned how..."
- "I investigated the relationship..."
- "I explored the tension between..."
- "I examined how..."

PROCESS LANGUAGE:
- "I experimented with..."
- "I pushed my work by..."
- "I discovered that..."
- "I revised my approach when..."

GROWTH LANGUAGE:
- "My understanding evolved..."
- "I moved from... to..."
- "This led me to realize..."
- "I synthesized my discoveries..."
```

### Statement Pitfalls

```
AVOID THESE ERRORS:

TOO VAGUE:
"I made art about identity."
(What about identity? How explored?)

TOO TECHNICAL:
"I used Photoshop layers and brushes."
(What was the inquiry? What discovered?)

NOT CONNECTED:
"I made these five pieces last semester."
(Where's the investigation? Connection?)

OVER THE LIMIT:
Count characters INCLUDING spaces!
Use a character counter tool.
```
