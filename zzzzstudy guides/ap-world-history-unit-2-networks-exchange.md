# üåç Unit 2: Networks of Exchange (1200-1450)

## üìö Unit Introduction

This unit examines how cross-cultural interactions intensified during 1200-1450, fundamentally transforming societies across Afro-Eurasia. The period saw the rise and fall of the largest land empire in history (the Mongols), the flourishing of Indian Ocean trade, and the devastating spread of the Black Death. Understanding these networks of exchange is essential for comprehending how the medieval world became increasingly interconnected! üåê

## ü¶† The Black Death: Complete Analysis

### Origins and Transmission

| Stage | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Origin** | Central Asian steppe (modern Kazakhstan/Mongolia) |
| **Cause** | Yersinia pestis bacterium |
| **Vector** | Fleas carried by rats |
| **Initial spread** | Mongol armies and trade caravans |
| **Entry to Europe** | Genoese trading ships from Caffa (1347) |

### Forms of Plague

| Type | Transmission | Symptoms | Mortality |
|------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| **Bubonic** | Flea bites | Swollen lymph nodes (buboes) | 50-70% |
| **Pneumonic** | Airborne | Lung infection; coughing blood | 90-95% |
| **Septicemic** | Blood infection | Skin blackening | Nearly 100% |

### Regional Mortality Estimates

| Region | Population Loss | Recovery Time |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Western Europe** | 30-60% | 150+ years |
| **Middle East** | 25-40% | Variable |
| **China** | 30-50% (combined with wars) | Yuan collapse |
| **Egypt** | 30-40% | Mamluk state weakened |
| **Sub-Saharan Africa** | Limited penetration | Minimal impact |

### Social and Economic Effects

| Area | Short-term Effect | Long-term Effect |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Labor** | Severe shortage | Higher wages; peasant power |
| **Serfdom** | Weakened | Declined in Western Europe |
| **Religion** | Increased piety; flagellants | Church authority questioned |
| **Persecution** | Jews scapegoated | Pogroms; expulsions |
| **Trade** | Temporary disruption | Restructured networks |
| **Art** | Death themes (danse macabre) | New cultural expressions |
| **Medicine** | Doctors helpless | Eventually spurred research |

### Long Essay Question (LEQ) Sample

**Prompt**: Evaluate the extent to which the Mongol Empire facilitated cultural exchange between 1200 and 1450.

**Thesis Framework**:
- Claim: Mongol Empire significantly facilitated cultural exchange through...
- Evidence 1: Protection of trade routes (Pax Mongolica)
- Evidence 2: Religious tolerance and patronage
- Evidence 3: Technology transfer (printing, gunpowder)
- Counter: Also spread disease; destroyed cultural centers

### Short Answer Question (SAQ) Sample

**Prompt**: 
a) Identify ONE technological innovation that spread along trade networks.
b) Explain how trade networks facilitated its spread.
c) Explain ONE effect of this technology's spread.

**Sample Answer**:
a) Gunpowder technology spread from China westward.
b) The Silk Road's revival under Mongol protection allowed Chinese innovations to reach the Middle East and eventually Europe. Mongol armies also employed Chinese engineers who brought this knowledge.
c) Gunpowder weapons eventually transformed warfare in Europe, contributing to the decline of feudal castles and armored knights, while strengthening centralized monarchies.
